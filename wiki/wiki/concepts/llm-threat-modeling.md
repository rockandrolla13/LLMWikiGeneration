# Threat Modeling for LLM Applications

## Definition

The application of classical threat-modeling discipline (identifying assets, trust boundaries, adversaries, and attack vectors) to LLM-powered systems, accounting for novel surfaces such as prompts, embeddings, retrieval stores, and plug-in tool calls. The book argues threat modeling must precede architecture choices like public-API vs privately-hosted models.
