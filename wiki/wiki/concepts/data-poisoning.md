# Data Poisoning

## Definition

A class of attack in which training, fine-tuning, or RAG-grounding data is deliberately tampered with to introduce vulnerabilities, biases, or backdoors that compromise the resulting model's security, accuracy, or ethical behavior. The Tay chatbot case study illustrates how unfiltered user input ingested as training data poisoned the model within hours.
