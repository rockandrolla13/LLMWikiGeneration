# Canonical Correlation Analysis

**Canonical Correlation Analysis (CCA)** is a method for finding linear combinations of two random vectors with maximum correlation; identifies the dimension and content of the common factor space between two groups.

## Sources

- [[sources/andreou-2020-mixed-frequency-macro-finance|Mixed-Frequency Macro-Finance Factor Models (2020)]] — supporting; used to test for and recover the dimensionality of the macro-finance common factor space
