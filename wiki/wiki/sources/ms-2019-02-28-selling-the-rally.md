# US Corporate Credit Strategy Brief: Selling the Rally

**Authors:** Adam S Richmond, Vishwas Patkar, Frederick T Fuchs, Felician G Stratmann, Jingli Li, Aleksandr Nozhnitskiy · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Having turned tactically constructive on US credit in early January, the team exits that buy recommendation and reverts to a longer-term cautious view, judging the risk/reward of remaining meaningfully long no longer attractive. They argue the January-February move was a bear-market rally, with IG/HY spreads 33bp/151bp tighter and sentiment having swung back to bullish. Their conviction is rising that spreads will trend wider as decade-long bull-market fundamentals deteriorate and the credit cycle slowly turns. They note that the bulk of spread widening historically occurs after the Fed finishes hiking, so a dovish Fed may bring the cycle turn closer.

## Key Claims

1. The early-2019 tactical buy recommendation is closed and the team reverts to a longer-term cautious view on US credit.
2. The January-February tightening is a bear-market rally; with spreads much tighter, long-term risks are less in-the-price.
3. Conviction is increasing that spreads will trend wider over the medium term as the credit cycle slowly turns.
4. Most cyclical spread widening occurs after the Fed finishes hiking, so an end to hikes may make a bigger cycle turn more likely.
