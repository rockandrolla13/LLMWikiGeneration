# US Corporate Credit Strategy: MS Credit-BMI Updated Reading

**Authors:** Meghan G. Robson, Adam S. Richmond, Vishwas Patkar, Frederick T. Fuchs, Felician G. Stratmann · **Year:** 2018 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley updates its Credit Bond Market Indicator (credit-BMI), a tactical framework combining volatility, momentum, sentiment, and liquidity signals into a single score from -10 (max short) to +10 (max long). The current reading of -1.4 places US credit in cautious territory but with low conviction. Weaker sentiment and elevated volatility support cautious positioning, while momentum and liquidity signals are neutral. The absolute level of the signal guides the degree of conviction for tactical recommendations.

## Key Claims

1. The credit-BMI reads -1.4, indicating cautious positioning with low conviction.
2. Each of four raw factor variables is converted to a z-score and mapped via a cumulative distribution function to a score from -10 to +10, then simple-averaged.
3. Weak signals near zero (between -1 and +1) are eliminated, and more extreme scores have higher success rates predicting credit excess return direction.
4. Sentiment (-2.3) and volatility (-1.5) drive the cautious reading, while momentum (-0.9) and liquidity (-0.7) are neutral.
