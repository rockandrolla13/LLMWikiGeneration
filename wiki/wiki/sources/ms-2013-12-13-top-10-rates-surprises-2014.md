# US Interest Rate Strategist: Top 10 Surprises for 2014

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach, Vipul Jain, Subadra Rajappa, Michael Ortiz · **Year:** 2013 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A year-end US Interest Rate Strategist note proposing ten potential surprises for liquid US interest rate markets in 2014, explicitly framed as risks to the base case rather than central forecasts. Scenarios span the yield curve, including UST 2-year yields ending above 2.00% on stronger growth and aggressive tapering, 10-year yields ending below 2.00% on muddle-through growth and disinflation, the 5s/10s curve breaching all-time highs, and TIPS pricing non-zero deflation probabilities. Further surprises cover swap spreads going fully negative, normalised 30-year skew, negative front-end rates from money-market fund reform, mortgage current-coupon OAS approaching zero, and up-in-coupon and 15s/30s underperformance.

## Key Claims

1. Stronger growth plus aggressive tapering could push UST 2-year yields above 2.00% by end-2014, bringing rate-hike expectations forward to January 2015.
2. A return to muddle-through growth and disinflation could drive UST 10-year yields below 2.00% and prompt additional Fed easing.
3. A cut to IOER plus a permanent unlimited RRP facility could drive the swap spread curve fully negative.
