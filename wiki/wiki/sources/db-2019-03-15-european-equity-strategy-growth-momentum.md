# European Equity Strategy: Global growth momentum starts to recover: what is priced in?

**Authors:** Sebastian Raedler, Andreas Bruckner, Thomas Pearce · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Deutsche Bank Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Deutsche Bank Research notes that global growth momentum began recovering in February 2019, with PMIs rising across China, the US, and the Euro area. The strategists argue the Stoxx 600 has overshot model-implied fair value (now ~8% above) and project European equities to underperform US equities by ~10% over the coming months, maintaining a long-standing underweight of Europe versus the US. They stay overweight European cyclicals versus defensives and overweight banks, while downgrading food & beverages and consumer durables to underweight on expected higher bond yields.

## Key Claims

1. Global PMI rose around 1 point in February 2019, the first improvement since August, led by a 3-point rebound in China manufacturing PMI.
2. The Stoxx 600 sits about 8% above model-implied fair value while the S&P 500 remains ~5% below, supporting a Europe-versus-US underweight.
3. Macro projections imply European equities underperform US equities by ~10% and cyclicals outperform defensives by a further ~9% by Q3.
4. Higher projected Bund (to 0.6%) and US 10y (to 3.2%) yields drive downgrades of food & beverages and consumer durables to underweight.
