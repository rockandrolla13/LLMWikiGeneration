# European Banks: CRR2 Agreed; ADI Boost for AT1

**Authors:** Jackie Ineke, Joe Hopkins · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley reviews the final CRR2/BRRD2 text, expecting Entry into Force in June 2019, and reads the six-year grandfathering of non-compliant capital instruments (to around June 2025) as a tailwind for AT1 valuations now that the market has repriced below par. MREL will largely be grandfathered to maturity, with the 8% subordination requirement applying only to G-SIBs and lower thresholds for other large banks. The team highlights a change to the ADI definition as a relief for Deutsche Bank, and notes regulatory capital must be under EU law or carry contractual bail-in recognition, with English-law bonds flagged given Brexit.

## Key Claims

1. CRR2 grandfathers non-compliant capital instruments for six years (to around June 2025), which becomes a tailwind for AT1 valuations after the market repriced below par.
2. MREL will be grandfathered until maturity in most cases, with 8% subordination applying only to G-SIBs and lower requirements for other banks over EUR 100bn assets.
3. The ADI definition will be changed, providing relief for Deutsche Bank.
4. Regulatory capital instruments must be written under EU law or carry contractual recognition of EU write-down powers, raising relevance for English-law bonds given Brexit.
