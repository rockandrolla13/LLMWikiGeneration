# Cross-Asset Playbook – February 2019: Fundamental Problems

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Wanting Low, Naomi Poole · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's February 2019 cross-asset allocation note argues that a dovish Federal Reserve shift boosted prices and sentiment, but masks real fundamental weakness in growth and earnings trends. The team maintains a defensive stance: equal-weight equities, equal-weight government bonds, underweight credit and overweight cash. They reiterate a 'turning points' thesis across the Broad USD, US vs. RoW equities, US vs. European duration and EM vs. corporate credit. Trade changes include closing long gold and EM local rates, switching a USDSEK short to a broad USD short, and adding a short UK inflation breakevens position.

## Key Claims

1. A dovish Fed lifted asset prices and sentiment despite genuine fundamental weakness in growth and earnings.
2. Inflationary pressures will make it harder for the Fed to sustain its dovish line.
3. The firm stays equal-weight equities and government bonds, underweight credit, and overweight cash.
4. Five key market relationships (notably Broad USD, US vs. RoW equities, US vs. European duration, EM vs. corporate credit) are at turning points.
