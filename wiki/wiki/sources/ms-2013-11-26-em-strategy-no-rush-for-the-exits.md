# EM Strategy Update: No Rush for the Exits

**Authors:** Rashique Rahman, Vandit D. Shah · **Year:** 2013 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

An EM strategy update arguing that fundamentals and valuations point to further asset-price re-pricing across EM currencies, local bonds and credit in coming months, but not a crisis given still-supportive market technicals. The authors see limited evidence of a broad-based rush out of EM assets despite persistent retail-oriented dedicated fund outflows. Institutional interest remains strong, with steady foreign ownership of local bonds and record third-party EM manager searches providing technical support for EM fixed income into 1Q14. Longer term, doubts about the sustainability of EM growth relative to DM could prompt even long-term investors to reassess EM allocations.

## Key Claims

1. Fundamentals and valuations argue for additional EM asset re-pricing across FX, local bonds and credit, but not a crisis given supportive technicals.
2. Despite persistent retail dedicated-fund outflows, institutional EM interest stays strong with steady foreign local-bond ownership and record manager searches.
3. Near-term technical support exists for EM fixed income into 1Q14 but moderates into 2Q14 as new-money mandates slow.

## Questions Raised

- Will concerns about the sustainability of EM growth relative to DM drive longer-term investors to reduce EM allocations?
