# Insight into the Balance Sheet

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach, Sam Elprince, Vishwanath Tirupattur, Chetan Ahya, Ellen Zentner, Adam S Richmond, Jay Bacow · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A US economics and fixed income strategy note arguing that Fed balance sheet normalization played an important role in the late-2018 market volatility, contrary to Chair Powell's view. The team frames the analysis around the stock effect, flow effect, and portfolio balance channel effect of QE and QT, identifying the portfolio balance channel as the most important factor today. It forecasts an end to balance sheet normalization in September 2019, which should lower estimates of future Treasury supply and yields.

## Key Claims

1. Balance sheet normalization, on its own and exacerbated by rate normalization, played an important role in late-2018 market volatility.
2. The Fed had already removed 9.5% of cumulative portfolio support through December 2018.
3. Morgan Stanley forecasts an end to balance sheet normalization in September 2019, with reinvestment caps phased out from July.
4. An earlier-than-expected end to normalization should reduce future Treasury supply estimates and lead to lower yields.
