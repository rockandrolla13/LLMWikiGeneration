# US Economics: Deeper Drop, Slower Climb

**Authors:** Ellen Zentner, Robert Rosener, Jan Kozak, Sarah A. Wolfe · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley economists revise down their US growth outlook amid the COVID-19 pandemic (the 'Great COVID-19 Recession'). They forecast a record 38% annualized GDP contraction in 2Q20, a slower 20.7% rebound in 3Q, and no return to pre-virus activity levels until end-2021. The unemployment rate is seen peaking at a record 15.7% in 2Q, with full-year 2020 GDP contracting 4.3% on a 4Q/4Q basis, the steepest postwar four-quarter drop.

## Key Claims

1. 2Q20 GDP is forecast to drop 38% annualized (vs -30% previously), with 1Q down 3.4%.
2. The 3Q recovery is revised down to a 20.7% annualized bounce, recovering only 35% of first-half lost output.
3. Unemployment is projected to peak at a record 15.7% in 2Q, with up to ~40 million job losses and a 28% rate in the bear case.
4. Full-year 2020 real GDP contracts 4.3% (4Q/4Q) and 5.5% on an annual-average basis, the steepest drop since 1946.
