# Factor Investing in the Corporate Bond Market

## Summary

Comprehensive study of Size, Low-Risk, Value, and Momentum factors in corporate bonds using Barclays US Corporate Investment Grade and High Yield indexes (Jan 1994 - Jun 2015). First paper to provide evidence on Size factor in bonds.

## Key Findings

- **All Four Factors Work**: Size, Low-Risk, Value, and Momentum generate significant alphas
- **Multi-Factor Benefits**: Low pairwise correlations enable diversification
- **Substantial Alphas**: 0.84% (IG) and 3.65% (HY) annualized Fama-French-Carhart alpha
- **Robust to Costs**: Break-even transaction costs well above actual costs

## Factor Definitions

| Factor | Selection Criteria |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Size** | Bonds from small companies (market value of outstanding bonds) |
| **Low-Risk** | Short maturity, high credit rating |
| **Value** | Credit spread high vs model-implied fair spread |
| **Momentum** | High past returns |

## Key Results

- Multi-factor portfolio: Higher IR, lower tracking error than single factors
- Bond factors add value beyond equity factors in multi-asset context
- Robust to alternative definitions, portfolio construction, liquid-only samples

## Contributions

1. **First Size Evidence**: No prior work on size factor in bonds
2. **Joint Analysis**: Consistent methodology across all four factors
3. **Multi-Asset**: Shows bond factors complement equity factors
