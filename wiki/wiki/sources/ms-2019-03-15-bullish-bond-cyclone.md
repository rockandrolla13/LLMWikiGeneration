# Global Interest Rate Strategist: The Bullish 'Bond Cyclone'

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach, Guneet Dhingra, Tony Small, Sam Elprince, David Harris, Shreya Chander, Robert J Brown, Koichi Sugisaki, Shoki Omori · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley stays bullish on duration in the US, Canada, and Japan, expecting US yields to fall further in a 'bond cyclone' after the Fed, and remains invested in the Ryder Cup of Bonds trade (US/Canada to outperform Germany/UK). The note argues the FOMC is positioned to cut rates to keep the expansion going if growth slows and inflation stays below 2%, and that priced-in 2020 cuts are likely to persist. It views ECB TLTRO-III as predominantly about Italy and Spain with minimal direct impact on periphery sovereign spreads, and maintains long-end JGB flatteners.

## Key Claims

1. US yields are expected to fall further in a bullish 'bond cyclone' as markets price the risk of pre-recession Fed rate cuts amid a fading fiscal impulse.
2. Priced-in 2020 rate cuts are likely to remain unless real GDP rebounds above potential and inflation moves higher.
3. ECB TLTRO-III is seen as predominantly about Italy and Spain and unlikely to materially move periphery sovereign spreads.
