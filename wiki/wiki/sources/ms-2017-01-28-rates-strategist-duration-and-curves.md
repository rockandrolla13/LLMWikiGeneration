# Global Interest Rate Strategist: Trading with Stocks – Duration and Curves

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach, Guneet Dhingra, Sam Elprince, Johanna Trost, Anton Heese, Elaine Lin, Federico Manicardi, Shreya Chander, Koichi Sugisaki, Kritika Kashyap, Jesper Rooth · **Year:** 2017 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's Global Interest Rate Strategist (January 28, 2017) turns bearish on duration across the G4 rates markets as rising equity-bond yield correlations and equity strength flipped its Bond Market Indicators (BMIs) negative. The team recommends staying short 10y gilts, holding Bund 5s30s steepeners, adding UST 5s30s steepeners into flattening, and receiving front-end rates in Australia and New Zealand. Sovereign, inflation, money-market and derivatives sections add tactical trades, including OAT-Bund 10s30s flatteners, a euro-area breakeven flattener, and selling TY puts versus swaption payors for zero cost.

## Key Claims

1. Rising correlations between equities and 10y government bond yields, plus equity strength, turned Morgan Stanley's Bond Market Indicators uniformly negative on duration across G4 markets.
2. The team recommends UST 5s30s steepeners, Bund 5s30s steepeners, short 10y gilts, and receiving front-end rates in Australia and New Zealand.
3. In the euro area, switching out of French and Italian 10s30s steepeners into OAT-Bund 10s30s flatteners is advised to protect against further spread widening.
4. Money-market repo rates are low due to MMF reform and reduced bill supply pushing money funds further into repo, with an uptick in bill issuance potentially lifting rates.
