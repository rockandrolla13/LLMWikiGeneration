# Global Volatility Playbook: What's Wrong with FX Volatility

**Authors:** Phanikiran Naraparaju, Sheena Shah, Wanting Low, Vishwas Patkar, Kelcie Gerson · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's monthly cross-asset volatility playbook for June 2019. Commodity vols (gold, Brent) and front-end rate vols spiked as the Fed signalled an easing bias, while G10 FX volatility (notably KRW and EUR) stayed stubbornly low and skew flattened across asset classes on a perceived Fed 'put'. The team argues easier policy is a pause rather than a new prolonged low-vol regime and that FX vols can still rise if the USD begins trending. Key trades include FX and equity worst-of-put correlation baskets, conditional JPY bull-flatteners, and a credit vol decompression trade buying HY puts funded with CDX IG puts.

## Key Claims

1. A spike in commodity volatility (gold and Brent) was the standout move for June 2019, even as equity and credit vols eased on a perceived Fed 'put'.
2. G10 FX volatility remained low because central banks turned proactively dovish in unison, preventing a strong USD trend; FX vols can still rise if the USD starts trending.
3. The 1-year 1-sigma tail for US 2yr rates is already priced at roughly 0.8% versus spot of 1.8%, reflecting widespread rate-cut signalling.
4. Easier policy is viewed as a pause in the volatility cycle rather than the start of a new prolonged low-vol backdrop, given expensive valuations and less slack than prior QE episodes.
