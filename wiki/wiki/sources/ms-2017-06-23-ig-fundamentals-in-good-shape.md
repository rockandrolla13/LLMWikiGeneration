# European Credit Strategy: IG Fundamentals — In Good Shape

**Authors:** Srikanth Sankaran, Max Blass, Aron Becker · **Year:** 2017 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley reviews 1Q17 financial statements for European IG corporates, finding continued divergence between European and US corporate behaviour as earnings momentum outpaces debt growth in Europe. European gross and net leverage declined to 2.8x and 1.8x while liquidity metrics and interest coverage stayed historically elevated, reflecting balance-sheet restraint. US 'Reverse Yankee' issuers in European markets show rising net leverage mirroring the broader US IG re-leveraging trend, and a sector framework favors telecoms over media, consumer discretionary and utilities.

## Key Claims

1. European IG gross and net leverage declined in 1Q17 to 2.8x and 1.8x respectively, extending a three-quarter deleveraging trend.
2. Liquidity remains historically elevated with median cash/debt near the 2014 record high of 30% and almost 40% of companies above 40% cash/debt.
3. Reverse Yankee (US) issuers in European credit show rising median net leverage, mirroring US IG re-leveraging and contrasting with European restraint.
4. Telecoms screens most favourably on fundamentals and valuation, while media, consumer discretionary and utilities screen least attractive.
