# AI Capex Funding: Data Center - Hyperscaler Bond Matrix Version 1.0

**Authors:** Tarek Hamid, Nathaniel Rosenbaum, Nelson Jantzen, Thomas Egan, Erica R. Spear, Silvi Mantri · **Year:** 2026 · **Venue:** J.P. Morgan North America Credit Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A J.P. Morgan North America Credit Research note (11 May 2026) mapping the rapidly expanding universe of corporate bonds tied to AI infrastructure: hyperscalers and the data center / HPC projects they lease. The combined universe spans 27 issuers with over $450bn outstanding. The core analytical contribution is a relative-value framework pairing each data-center/HPC bond against its hyperscaler lessor (average spread relationship 105bp for IG-rated projects, 183bp for HY). The note examines index-inclusion mechanics (arguing the 144A-for-life exclusion is outdated), introduces a Data Center subsector in J.P. Morgan's JULI index, and documents explosive HPC HY issuance ($26.6bn YTD) and outperformance (+9.99% YTD vs +1.61% for HY).

## Key Claims

1. The hyperscaler + data center bond universe spans 27 issuers with over $450bn outstanding across HG and HY indices.
2. Average spread relationship between a hyperscaler and an IG-rated leased project is 105bp; for HY-rated projects, 183bp.
3. Widest hyperscaler-to-IG-project relationship is BBB Oracle (145bp); tightest is AA Meta (36bp).
4. Most data-center securities are excluded from key benchmark indices due to 144A-for-life status and limited syndication.
5. J.P. Morgan argues the 144A-for-life exclusion is outdated given today's QIB-dominated, fund/ETF-based bond buying.
6. HPC (AI) HY issuance totals $26.6bn YTD (43% of non-refi HY issuance); the subsector grew to 2.68% of the JPMorgan HY Bond index.
7. HPC returned +9.99% YTD versus +1.61% for the broader HY index, a major source of alpha.

## Questions Raised

- How should investors price guarantee/security differences between leasing from a project that issues its own bonds versus a hyperscaler issuing its own?
- What objective criteria should index providers adopt to handle limited-syndication deals consistently?
- Can the rapid HPC-driven HY issuance pace and tight spreads persist, or do they signal concentration risk?
