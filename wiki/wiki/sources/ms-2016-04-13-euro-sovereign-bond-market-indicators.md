# Global Interest Rate Strategy: Euro Sovereign Bond Market Indicators (eBMIs)

**Authors:** Jesper Rooth, Anton Heese, Matthew Hornbach · **Year:** 2016 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley extends its suite of systematic Bond Market Indicators (BMIs) to euro sovereign spreads, introducing eBMIs that guide tactical calls on semi-core and peripheral spreads versus core (France, Italy, Spain at the 10-year point). Unlike the original duration-focused BMIs, eBMIs predict credit-spread returns, requiring different factor rationales because credit tightens with improving sentiment and exhibits fat-tailed, blow-up-prone return distributions. Factors include volatility-adjusted carry, spread momentum, and government bond supply (exploiting a V-shaped return pattern around issuance). The note also reviews the original BMIs on their one-year anniversary, finding strong out-of-sample performance overall though disappointing in JGBs, and offers a tactical trade to sell BTP versus OAT futures.

## Key Claims

1. The new eBMIs predict euro sovereign credit-spread returns rather than duration returns, requiring inverted factor rationales and safeguards against blow-up losses.
2. eBMI factors include volatility-adjusted carry, spread momentum (driven by a credit-funding feedback loop), and bond-supply timing around issuance concessions.
3. First-year live BMI performance was broadly in line with back-tests, better than expected in USTs but disappointing in JGBs.
