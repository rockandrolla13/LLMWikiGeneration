# A Birdie for the Balance Sheet — Duration and Curves

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach, Guneet Dhingra, Tony Small, Sam Elprince, David Harris, Shreya Chander, Robert J Brown, Koichi Sugisaki, Shoki Omori · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A Global Interest Rate Strategist note focused on the upcoming FOMC meeting and the Fed's balance sheet normalization policy. The team stays neutral on US duration and the yield curve but looks to add duration on auction-related concessions, while exiting several euro-area periphery curve trades (high- vs low-coupon BTP, 10s30s BonoBund flattener, 2s5s BTP-Bund flattener) and maintaining a bullish JGB duration bias. It covers sovereign auction supply, US inflation/TIPS positioning, UK RPI reform, and US money-market spreads (Libor-OIS, swap spreads). Morgan Stanley Bond Market Indicators (BMI(10)) read neutral across the G4 but bullish in Australia and New Zealand.

## Key Claims

1. As Fed communication signals an early end to balance-sheet normalization, US Treasuries should continue to outperform other global bonds.
2. The team stays neutral on US duration and curve, looking to buy 5y (2.60-2.75%), 7y (2.70-2.85%), and 10y (2.75-2.90%) notes on auction concessions.
3. Momentum in bond-market excess returns is the most supportive factor for global duration, with vol-adjusted carry most supportive in Australia and New Zealand.
4. They exit euro-area periphery curve trades and maintain a periphery-outperformance theme, plus a bullish JGB duration bias.
