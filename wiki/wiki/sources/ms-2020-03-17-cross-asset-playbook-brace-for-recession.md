# Cross-Asset Playbook – March 2020: Brace for Recession

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Wanting Low, Naomi Poole, Serena Tang · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's March 2020 cross-asset note (dated March 17, 2020) argues the global COVID-19 outbreak will tip the US and global economies into recession, the final leg of a late-cycle downturn that predated the virus, with policy easing able to dampen but not offset the trend. Amid extreme volatility, the team judges strategic risk/reward is gradually improving as prices fall, moving to neutral across global equities, credit and government bonds while reducing cautious US views. For the first time in two years they turn net sellers of cross-asset volatility, believing vol premiums are most excessive and need to stabilise before broader markets recover. Trade changes include adding Agency MBS vs. Treasury and selling equity and FX vol.

## Key Claims

1. The COVID-19 outbreak will push the US and global economies into recession.
2. More policy easing can dampen but not offset the late-cycle downturn.
3. Strategic risk/reward is gradually improving as prices fall; the team moves toward neutral across equities, credit and government bonds.
4. For the first time in two years, the team is a net seller of cross-asset volatility, where risk premiums look most excessive.
