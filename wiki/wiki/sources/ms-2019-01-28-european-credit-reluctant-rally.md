# European Credit Playbook: A Reluctant Rally

**Authors:** Srikanth Sankaran, Max Blass, Aron Becker · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley reviews the strong January 2019 rally in European credit, particularly high yield, following the December 2018 sell-off. The strategists view the catch-up as partly justified but not an 'all clear' signal, with dovish central bank rhetoric offsetting soft economic data. They recommend staying liquid in cash credit and nimble in indices, favoring short-dated bonds, short-call hybrids and select AT1s while flagging fundamental and liquidity challenges for smaller BBB and HY issuers.

## Key Claims

1. January 2019 was on track to be among the strongest months for European HY excess returns in a decade after the December 2018 damage.
2. Dovish central bank rhetoric has more than offset soft economic data, with corporate earnings seen as the key near-term catalyst.
3. The case for adding beta in cash portfolios is weak; staying low in duration and up in quality is preferred, favoring sub-5-year paper, short-call hybrids and select AT1s over single-Bs.
4. Fundamental and liquidity challenges for smaller BBB and HY issuers remain key concerns.
