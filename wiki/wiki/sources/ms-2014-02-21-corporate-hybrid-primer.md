# Credit Strategy: Corporate Hybrid Primer - 2014 Edition

**Authors:** Serena Tang, Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Shrina B Poojara · **Year:** 2014 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley European Credit Strategy note (February 21, 2014), a primer on corporate hybrid bonds whose structures and call language may be unfamiliar to many credit investors. The market has roughly doubled in size over the prior year as corporates lock in low yields with equity benefit and credit investors hunt for yield. The note introduces a hybrid relative value model centered on 'hybrid compensation' (the spread for options sold to the issuer) and convexity. Strategically, the team is less constructive as hybrid-specific compensation has shrunk, but views hybrids as preferable beta for High Yield and allocation-constrained investors, while preferring banks subordinated debt and increasingly favoring relative value trades.

## Key Claims

1. The corporate hybrid market doubled in size over the prior year, driven by issuers locking in low yields and investors hunting for yield.
2. 'Hybrid compensation' - the spread investors receive for options sold to the issuer - is crucial to corporate perpetual valuation, along with convexity.
3. Hybrid-specific compensation has normalised/shrunk, making the team less constructive on the asset class.
4. Hybrids offer preferable beta for High Yield and allocation-constrained investors, but the team prefers banks subordinated (Tier 1) debt.
