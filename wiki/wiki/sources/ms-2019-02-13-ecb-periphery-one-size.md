# One Size Doesn't Fit All

**Authors:** Pasquale M Diana, Georgi Deyanov, Matthew Pennill, Gek Teng Khoo, Filip Denchev, James K Lord · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley cross-asset note (February 13, 2019) arguing that a softer euro area outlook implies a more dovish ECB, but investors underestimate how diverse the 'ECB periphery' (CEE, Scandi, Switzerland) is. The economics team expects the ECB to hold rates well into 2020 and hike only once in June 2020, and groups seven central banks into normalisers (CNB, NBH, NBR), hesitant hikers (Norges Bank, Riksbank), and those holding back (NBP, SNB). In rates and FX, CHF/SEK/NOK yields should see larger upside than CEE on closer ECB dependency, with CZK the top CEE pick, Scandi FX favoured, and CHF a preferred short. Equity strategy stays Overweight Poland and Czech Republic and cautious on Swedish property.

## Key Claims

1. The ECB is expected to keep rates on hold well into 2020 and raise rates only once, in June 2020.
2. The 'ECB periphery' is highly diverse, splitting into normalisers (CNB, NBH, NBR), hesitant hikers (Norges Bank, Riksbank), and those holding back (NBP, SNB).
3. If EUR yields rise, CHF, SEK and NOK yields should see larger upside than CEE yields due to closer ECB dependency.
4. CZK is the top CEE FX pick on rising rates, Scandi FX should appreciate, and CHF is a preferred short.
