# European Banks: Autumn AT1 Primer – Extension and Par Calls

**Authors:** Joe Hopkins, Jackie Ineke · **Year:** 2018 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's AT1 primer argues extension is the key risk for Additional Tier 1 bonds, with spread widening pushing 2017/early-2018 call options out of the money, but contends some high-quality low-reset bonds have been oversold and are better valued to a second or third call date than to perpetuity. Coupon risk (MDA/ADI driven) is judged low for European banks and trigger risk is remote outside resolution, given an average buffer of EUR 12.6bn (9.7% of RWAs) to trigger. The greater concern is early par-call risk on high cash-price bonds: regulatory par calls tied to the CRR2 timeline for non-EU-law bonds, plus tax par-call optionality for Italian, Portuguese, Danish and Spanish AT1.

## Key Claims

1. Extension risk is the key risk for AT1 as spread widening pushes 2017/early-2018 call options out of the money, but some high-quality low-reset bonds look oversold when valued to a second/third call date rather than perpetuity.
2. MDA-linked coupon risk is low for European banks and trigger risk is remote outside resolution, with the average issuer holding a buffer of EUR 12.6bn (9.7% of RWAs) to its trigger.
3. Early par-call risk makes high cash-price bonds vulnerable: regulatory par calls depend on the CRR2 timeline for non-EU-law bonds lacking contractual bail-in recognition.
4. Tax par-call optionality is a risk for Italian, Portuguese, Danish and Spanish AT1.
