# Global Macro Commentary | April 2

**Authors:** Edward von der Schmidt, Matthew Hornbach, Andrew M Watrous, Gilberto A Hernandez-Gomez, Sarah A Wolfe, Filip Denchev, Gek Teng Khoo, Alina Zaytseva, Shoki Omori, Belle Chang · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Daily global macro commentary covering April 2, 2020, during the early COVID-19 market turmoil. Equities and crude rallied on the prospect of oil production cuts, overshadowing a record 6.65mm weekly US unemployment claims. Treasuries absorbed increased coupon sizes and a record weekly slate of IG issuance (~$110.9bn in four days), while the USD and petrocurrencies strengthened. The note tracks central bank and government policy responses across the US, Europe, and Asia.

## Key Claims

1. US weekly jobless claims hit a record 6.648mm, doubling the prior week and bringing the two-week total near 10mm.
2. IG issuance reached a record weekly total of $110.9bn in just four days, weighing on the Treasury curve.
3. Prospect of an oil output truce lifted WTI crude 24.7% to $25.32/bbl, leading a 9% advance in energy stocks.
4. The Fed's SLR announcement and supportive policy headlines softened the risk-off tone despite dire macro data.
