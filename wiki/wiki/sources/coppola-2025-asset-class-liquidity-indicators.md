# Asset Class Liquidity Risk Indicators: Timing the Risk in European and US Equity and Bond Markets

**Authors:** [[entities/anna-coppola|Anna Coppola]], [[entities/giovanni-urga|Giovanni Urga]], [[entities/alessandro-varaldo|Alessandro Varaldo]]

**Venue:** *Journal of Financial Stability*, 76, 101369 (2025)

**DOI:** [10.1016/j.jfs.2024.101369](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jfs.2024.101369)

## Summary

Constructs [[concepts/liquidity-risk|liquidity-risk]] indicators for six European and US equity and bond markets (2007-2023) by aggregating financial, monetary, and credit variables through [[concepts/ecdf-aggregation|ECDF standardization]]. Uses endogenous [[concepts/markov-switching-models|Markov-switching models]] to identify low/medium/high-risk regimes and maps these regimes to bubble episodes detected via recursive (PSY-style) [[concepts/bubble-detection|bubble-detection]] testing procedures. The framework distinguishes [[concepts/funding-liquidity|funding liquidity]] from market liquidity in the spirit of [[entities/markus-brunnermeier|Brunnermeier]]-Pedersen and uses [[concepts/amihud-illiquidity|Amihud illiquidity]] as a building block.

## Key Claims

1. Aggregating monetary and credit variables with traditional financial liquidity proxies sharpens detection of stress
2. Endogenous Markov-switching models pinpoint stress episodes including the GFC, 2011-12 sovereign crisis, 2015-16 Chinese crash, COVID, and 2022 Ukraine invasion
3. Time-varying contribution analysis shows credit drivers dominate during the sovereign crisis and monetary drivers during COVID
