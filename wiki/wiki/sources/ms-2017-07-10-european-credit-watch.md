# European Credit Strategy — What We're Watching

**Authors:** Srikanth Sankaran, Max Blass, Aron Becker · **Year:** 2017 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A weekly European credit strategy data note from Morgan Stanley. The week saw a continued coordinated sell-off in developed market government bonds, which the firm's rates strategists attribute to positioning shifts rather than a reassessment of the monetary policy outlook. The traditional negative correlation between credit spreads and bond yields reasserted itself, with EUR IG spreads tightening 4bp while HY widened 9bp amid weak summer liquidity. The note tracks cash and CDS index performance, credit valuation, and fund flows across euro, sterling and US corporate markets.

## Key Claims

1. The recent Bund sell-off was driven by positioning shifts rather than a substantial reassessment of the monetary policy outlook.
2. The negative correlation between credit spreads and bond yields reasserted itself, with EUR IG spreads tightening 4bp and financials outperforming non-financials.
3. EUR IG mutual funds saw flat net flows while HY funds saw outflows equivalent to 1.7% of AUM.
