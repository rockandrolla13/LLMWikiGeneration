# EM Quant Strategy: EMFX Quant's Lab - Carry Performs

**Authors:** Andres Jaime · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's EMFX quant note reports that the PortHB high-beta allocation rose to 33%, above the 25% threshold to signal long EM local-currency exposure, though the signal has been inconsistent since mid-February, indicating a lack of EM momentum. The regime-switching (RSM) indicator remains neutral at 47%, with VIX and US credit the main contributors and room to rise before signalling extreme pricing. Carry, alongside VIRP, has been the best-performing factor year-to-date, with the leveraged portfolio returning roughly 6% YTD after carry and transaction costs at an 8% target vol. Positioning trimmed MXN and BRL while adding INR, IDR and PLN, and stays underweight CNY.

## Key Claims

1. The PortHB high-beta allocation rose to 33%, above the 25% threshold signalling long EM local-currency exposure.
2. Carry (with VIRP) was the best-performing factor YTD, with the leveraged portfolio returning ~6% YTD at 8% target vol.
3. The RSM regime-switching indicator remained neutral at 47%, driven mainly by VIX and US credit.
4. Positioning trimmed MXN and BRL and added INR, IDR and PLN, staying underweight CNY.
