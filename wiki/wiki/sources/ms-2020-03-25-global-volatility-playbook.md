# Global Volatility Playbook: A Market of Many Extremes

**Authors:** Phanikiran Naraparaju, Sheena Shah, Wanting Low, Vishwas Patkar, Ailing Deng, Andres Jaime, Serena Tang · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A Global Derivatives Strategy playbook documenting that every type of volatility risk premium - implied vols, skew, and term structures - reached extremes during the COVID-19 pandemic selloff across equities, FX, and fixed income. The team argues volatility should peak before the final risk-markets trough and recommends selling S&P 500 and USDJPY strangles to harvest elevated premium. It also favors CDX IG 1x2 receiver spreads and HY/IG decompression to position for central-bank-supported credit, and selling front-end rates vol while buying longer expiries to normalize extreme calendar inversion.

## Key Claims

1. Every category of volatility risk premium (implied vol, skew, term structure) hit extremes across asset classes on pandemic concerns.
2. Volatility is expected to peak before risk markets reach their final trough.
3. Selling S&P 500 and USDJPY 3m 25d strangles captures rich premium with wide breakeven ranges.
4. IG credit benefits most from central bank support, favoring CDX IG 1x2 receiver spreads and HY/IG decompression hedged with IG puts.
