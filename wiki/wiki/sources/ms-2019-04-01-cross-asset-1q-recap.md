# Global In the Flow: First Quarter Recap

**Authors:** Wanting Low, Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Naomi Poole, Serena Tang · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's cross-asset strategy team recaps a strong first quarter of 2019, in which the S&P 500 posted its best 1Q since 1998 (+13.6%), 10-year US Treasuries gained 2.9%, Bund yields fell below zero, and credit spreads tightened broadly. Returns were highly front-loaded and slowed materially in March. Technicals showed weak gross issuance in DM high yield (-15% YoY) and DM sovereigns (-11% YoY), while sentiment indicators (GRDI falling to -0.1, retail sentiment turning positive) prompted a cautious stance. The market was pricing in Fed rate cuts over the next 12 months.

## Key Claims

1. The S&P 500 gained 13.6% in 1Q19, its strongest first quarter since 1998, with returns concentrated early in the quarter.
2. Markets were pricing in Federal Reserve rate cuts over the next 12 months as of the end of 1Q19.
3. Gross issuance fell year-over-year in DM high yield (-15%) and DM sovereigns (-11%), while DM IG and EM were roughly flat.
