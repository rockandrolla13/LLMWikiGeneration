# Cross-Asset Dispatches: Volatility Peaks Before Markets Trough

**Authors:** Phanikiran L Naraparaju, Andrew Sheets, Serena W Tang, Wanting Low, Naomi Z Poole · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley argues that the March 2020 COVID-driven volatility repricing across rates, credit, commodities and currencies is historic and broad-based, with 3-month implied levels and skew at extremes rarely seen even in 2008. Drawing on the 2008, 2011, 2015 and 2018 episodes, they contend that volatility typically peaks before markets reach their final trough, as investors first grow comfortable with the level of uncertainty and only then with price. They view risk/reward as improving and recommend selling S&P 500 and USDJPY 3m 25d strangles to harvest elevated premium. They also favour covered-short structures.

## Key Claims

1. Cross-asset volatility (rates, credit, commodities, currencies) reached extreme levels, with the S&P 500 posting seven consecutive days of >4% moves, beating the 1929 record.
2. Volatility tends to peak before the ultimate market trough, as seen in 2008, 2011, 2015 and 2018.
3. Risk/reward for risk assets is improving but volatility stabilisation remains a key unticked box.
4. Selling S&P 500 and USDJPY 3m 25d strangles captures historically rich option premium.
