# European Credit Strategy: A Premium for Size

**Authors:** Srikanth Sankaran, Max Blass, Aron Becker · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley studies how issuer size affects secondary liquidity and bond performance in European IG credit. They find the liquidity advantage of 'mega cap' issuers is overestimated while 'small cap' issuers offer a generous premium for hold-to-maturity investors. Using custom size-based indices built from the iBoxx EUR IG universe, they show that normalised bid-ask spreads converge during stress, undermining the mega cap liquidity edge, with size effects stronger within financials.

## Key Claims

1. Of 582 issuers in the iBoxx EUR IG index, 40 'mega caps' with over EUR 12bn outstanding account for 36% of the index balance, driven by QE-era non-financial growth.
2. 170 small-cap issuers with under EUR 1bn of index-eligible bonds have seen their relative weight stay flat over five years despite rising in number.
3. Mega cap liquidity advantage holds in normal conditions but normalised bid-ask spreads converge during stress, undermining the benefit of holding mega caps.
4. Small caps offer a generous illiquidity premium attractive to hold-to-maturity investors; size effects on liquidity are far more significant within financials.
