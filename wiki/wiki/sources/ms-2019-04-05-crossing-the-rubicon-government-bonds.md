# Crossing the Rubicon (Government Bonds)

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach, Guneet Dhingra, Tony Small, Sam Elprince, David Harris, Shreya Chander, Robert J Brown, Koichi Sugisaki, Shoki Omori · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's Global Interest Rate Strategist notes that market pricing of Fed policy has crossed the 'Rubicon' from pricing rate hikes to pricing rate cuts over the next 12 months, and expects that cut pricing to persist. They recommend positioning long duration in the US and Canada against short duration in Europe, staying neutral on US duration and curve while favouring euro-area periphery tightening via long 50/50 10y PGBs/Bonos versus short 10y French OATs. UK trades include 30s40s steepeners and 2s5s GBP swap steepeners, and in Japan they remain constructive on the long end and maintain a JGB 20s40s flattener. On inflation they like long US 5-year real yields for risk-adjusted carry.

## Key Claims

1. Fed policy pricing shifted from rate hikes to rate cuts over the next 12 months and is expected to persist.
2. Position long duration in the US and Canada against short duration in Europe.
3. Favour euro-area periphery tightening via long 50/50 10y PGBs/Bonos vs short 10y French OATs.
4. Long US 5-year real yields offer attractive risk-adjusted carry over the next three months.
