# Differentiation and Divergence

**Authors:** Sivan Mahadevan, Christopher R Metli, Peter Mallik, Jay Sole, Praveen Singh, Viktor Hjort, Gaurav Rastogi · **Year:** 2011 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A Global Derivatives Strategy report whose theme is differentiation and divergence across markets, opening with cross-asset skew divergence as a signal of differing perceived risks. It lays out a constructive 2011 global growth base case (4.2% GDP) despite the Japan quake and elevated oil, with global rebalancing as a key longer-term theme and rising inflation risks. The note tracks monetary policy normalization beginning at the ECB and discusses the euro-area sovereign debt crisis and peripheral bond differentiation, alongside cross-asset, equity, and credit derivatives trade themes.

## Key Claims

1. Cross-asset volatility skew has diverged, indicating markets are pricing differing risks across regions and assets.
2. The base case is constructive on global growth (2011 GDP 4.2%) despite the Japan earthquake and elevated oil prices.
3. Monetary policy normalization is starting at the ECB, with a first Fed hike priced for 1Q 2012 and a BoE hike expected in August.
4. Peripheral euro-area bond markets are clearly differentiating between Greece/Ireland/Portugal and Spain.
