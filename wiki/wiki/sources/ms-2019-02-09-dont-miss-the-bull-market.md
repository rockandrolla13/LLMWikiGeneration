# Global Interest Rate Strategist: Don't Miss the Bull Market

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach, Guneet Dhingra, Tony Small, Sam Elprince, David Harris, Shreya Chander, Robert J Brown, Koichi Sugisaki, Shoki Omori · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley argues investors should not turn bearish on bonds despite the 2019 Fed pause, noting that monetary policy is tighter than in 2016 and will tighten further via balance sheet normalization and lagged hike effects. The firm favors duration longs (notional-neutral 2s5s flatteners in the US, a Portugal/Spain vs France basket in the euro area) and maintains bullish long-end JGB calls. Bond Market Indicators (BMI(10)) are neutral while BMI(2) front-end signals have turned negative on the US, Germany, and UK due to equity strength and poor carry.

## Key Claims

1. The 2019 Fed pause differs from 2016 because US monetary policy is materially tighter now and balance sheet normalization plus lagged effects of 2018 hikes keep conditions tightening.
2. BMI(10) signals are neutral across markets while BMI(2) front-end models have turned negative for the US, Germany, and the UK on equity strength and weak front-end carry.
3. Preferred expressions include US 2s5s flatteners, a 50:50 Portugal/Spain 10y vs France basket, and JGB 20s40s flatteners with long-end duration.
