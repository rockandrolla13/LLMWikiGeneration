# Global In the Flow: 2018 by the Numbers

**Authors:** Wanting Low, Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Serena Tang, Naomi Poole · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's January 2, 2019 'Global In the Flow' note reviews 2018 as a tough year in which nearly all major assets posted negative returns, some worse than 2008, with virtually no major asset class beating inflation. The year featured an unusually large number of 3-sigma moves and surprises, including the largest one-day VIX spike on record and the worst December for the S&P 500 since the 1930s. Technicals show negative year-on-year net issuance in DM IG, HY and EM credit while DM sovereign issuance stayed positive. Sentiment indicators moved toward fear, with the MS Global Risk Demand Index falling to -2.98 and VIX reaching 36% in December.

## Key Claims

1. Nearly all major assets posted negative 2018 returns, with virtually none beating inflation.
2. 2018 saw the worst December S&P 500 returns since the 1930s and worst December US credit excess returns since 1989.
3. DM IG, HY and EM credit net issuance turned negative year-on-year while DM sovereign issuance stayed positive.
4. Sentiment moved toward fear, with the MS Global Risk Demand Index at -2.98 and VIX at 36% intra-December.
