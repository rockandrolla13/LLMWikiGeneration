# European Banks: Facing the Cycle – Picking Swiss

**Authors:** Jackie Ineke, Joe Hopkins · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

With AT1s down 7-16 points since February highs amid the Covid-19 selloff and no large buy-side stepping in, Morgan Stanley recommends tentative first steps into a few AT1s trading wider than January 2019 spreads, specifically Swiss low-trigger names. MS economists now expect an ECB rate cut alongside mitigation measures (higher tiering exemption multiplier, extra QE, new TLTROs), with euro area 2020 growth cut to 0.4% and a 1H recession. The team favours up-in-quality positioning and buys USD low-trigger Swiss AT1s (Credit Suisse 7.5% Dec-23 and UBS 7% Feb-25), citing strong underlying credits, low 7% MDA levels and a history of first-call redemption.

## Key Claims

1. AT1s fell 7-16 points from February highs in the Covid-19 selloff without large buy-side participation, prompting tentative re-entry into names wider than January 2019 spreads.
2. MS economists expect an ECB rate cut (10bp) plus mitigation via higher tiering exemption, extra QE (~EUR 20bn/month) and new TLTROs, with euro area 2020 growth cut to 0.4% and a 1H recession.
3. The team recommends up-in-quality Swiss low-trigger AT1s (Credit Suisse 7.5% Dec-23, UBS 7% Feb-25), citing strong credits, a low 7% MDA level and consistent first-call redemption.
4. Around 10% of AT1s trade cheap to late-2018/early-2019 spread wides, a condition needed to improve the broader credit valuation case.
