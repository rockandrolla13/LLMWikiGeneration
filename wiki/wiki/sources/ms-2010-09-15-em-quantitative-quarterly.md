# Emerging Markets Quantitative Quarterly: EM Fixed Income and Foreign Exchange Strategy

**Authors:** Rogerio Oliveira, Rashique Rahman, Paolo Batori, Chuan Lim, Juha Seppala · **Year:** 2010 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

The first edition of Morgan Stanley's Emerging Markets Quantitative Quarterly (EMQQ), a compendium of the quantitative models and methodologies developed by the EM and AXJ Strategy groups, organized into credit, rates and currencies sections plus a cross-asset category. Credit pieces cover sovereign debt sustainability frameworks (simulating debt-to-GDP paths) and a sovereign ranking/credit model (SRCM and SovRank). Rates work analyzes tightening cycles and curve flattening across CEEMEA and Latin America plus a Brazil rates risk-premia model, while currency pieces value fair EMFX implied volatility and hedging of FX inconvertibility risk. The publication aims to be a durable reference for the models, assumptions and numerical procedures behind EM trade ideas rather than a snapshot of current results.

## Key Claims

1. EMQQ compiles the quantitative models behind EM trade ideas across credit, rates, currencies and cross-asset domains as a one-stop methodological reference.
2. The sovereign debt-sustainability model simulates future debt-to-GDP paths from inputs including initial debt ratios, interest rates, growth forecasts and primary surplus.
3. Rates analyses link central-bank tightening cycles to curve flattening across CEEMEA and Latin America, complemented by a Brazil rates risk-premia model.
