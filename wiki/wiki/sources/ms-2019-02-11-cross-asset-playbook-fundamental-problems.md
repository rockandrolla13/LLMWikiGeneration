# Cross-Asset Playbook – February 2019: Fundamental Problems

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Wanting Low, Naomi Poole · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

The February 2019 Cross-Asset Strategy Playbook argues that despite a dovish Fed shift boosting prices and sentiment, real fundamental weakness persists in growth and earnings trends, with inflationary pressures making it harder for the Fed to hold its line. The team stays EW equities, EW government bonds, UW credit and OW cash, looking for turning points in the broad USD, US vs RoW equity performance, US vs European duration, and EM vs corporate credit. It reduces EM local exposure after strong gains and raises cash. Trades lower top-trade equity beta from 0.4x to 0.1x, close long gold and long EM local rates, switch a USDSEK short into a broad USD short, and add a short UK inflation breakevens position.

## Key Claims

1. A dovish Fed boosted prices and sentiment, but real fundamental weakness in growth and earnings persists alongside inflationary pressure.
2. The team stays EW equities, EW government bonds, UW credit and OW cash, awaiting better fundamentals, more bearish sentiment, or better valuations.
3. 2019 is expected to mark turning points in the broad USD, US vs RoW equities, US vs European duration, and EM vs corporate credit.
4. Top-trade equity beta is lowered from 0.4x to 0.1x by closing long gold and EM local rates and switching USDSEK short into a broad USD short.
