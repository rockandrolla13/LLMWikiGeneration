# Cross-Asset Dispatches: What Do Recoveries Usually Look Like?

**Authors:** Serena W Tang, Naomi Z Poole, Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran L Naraparaju, Wanting Low · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley examines cross-asset sequencing around prior bear-market lows to gauge what a recovery from the March 23, 2020 low might look like, reiterating an overweight in credit. They note the COVID sell-off and rebound were outliers in speed, severity and sector leadership, and that global equities historically take about 15 months to recover prior peaks, with V-shaped recoveries rare. ACWI rallies a median 35% in the 12 months after a trough, though the link between sell-off depth and recovery strength is weaker than commonly assumed. Around troughs, bond yields typically decline, credit spreads widen, and oil and gold rally, with defensives outperforming into the low and lagging in the rally.

## Key Claims

1. Global equities historically take roughly 15 months to recover prior peaks after a bear-market low; V-shaped recoveries are rare.
2. On median, ACWI rallies 35% in the 12 months following a trough.
3. Global defensive sectors outperform into the trough and underperform as equities rally.
4. Into equity troughs bond yields tend to fall, credit spreads widen and oil and gold rally.
