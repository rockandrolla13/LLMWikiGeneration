# Information Flows in Trading Networks

**Authors:** [[entities/stefan-huber|Stefan J. Huber]], [[entities/edward-watts|Edward M. Watts]], [[entities/christina-zhu|Christina Zhu]]

**Venue:** *Journal of Accounting and Economics*, in press (2026)

**DOI:** [10.1016/j.jacceco.2026.101876](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jacceco.2026.101876)

## Summary

Uses regulatory insurance-company transaction data on US [[concepts/corporate-bonds|corporate bonds]] to map insurer-[[concepts/dealer-networks|dealer trading networks]] and shows that insurers with larger dealer networks earn superior risk-adjusted returns by trading ahead of downgrades, defaults, and M&A events. The performance advantage concentrates in connections to dealers most likely to have private credit-relevant information, consistent with [[concepts/informed-trading|informed trading]] propagating through [[concepts/social-networks-in-finance|social networks in finance]] in [[concepts/otc-markets|OTC markets]].

## Key Claims

1. Larger dealer networks predict more profitable trading by insurers around adverse credit events, with monthly high-low risk-adjusted returns of about 7 bps
2. Performance is concentrated in ties to dealers with research/underwriting roles or M&A involvement, consistent with private-information leakage through trading relationships
3. Quid-pro-quo behavior is detectable: insurers reward informative dealers with future order flow
