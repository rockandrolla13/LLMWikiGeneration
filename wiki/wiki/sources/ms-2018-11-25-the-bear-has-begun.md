# 2019 US Credit Outlook: The Bear Has Begun

**Authors:** Adam S Richmond, Vishwas Patkar, Frederick T Fuchs, Felician G Stratmann · **Year:** 2018 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

The 2019 US credit outlook argues a credit bear market began when IG spreads hit cycle tights in February 2018 and will continue, with HY and then loans underperforming as headwinds shift from technicals to fundamentals. The team sees a more challenging macro backdrop in 2019 as Fed policy nears restrictive territory and economic and earnings growth decelerate, turning late-cycle into end-of-cycle risk. Base-case forecasts include a -0.8% IG excess return, 0.5% HY total return, 1.3% loan total return and a 2.9% HY default rate. They recommend moving up-in-quality, using rallies to de-risk, and specific IG, HY and derivatives positioning.

## Key Claims

1. A credit bear market began when IG spreads hit cycle tights in Feb 2018 and will continue into 2019.
2. Headwinds shift from technicals (flows, liquidity) to fundamentals (credit quality, slowing growth) as late-cycle risk becomes end-of-cycle fear.
3. Base-case 2019 forecasts: -0.8% IG excess return, 0.5% HY total return, 1.3% loan total return, 2.9% HY default rate.
4. Investors should use rallies to move up-in-quality until valuations price long-term fundamental risks.
