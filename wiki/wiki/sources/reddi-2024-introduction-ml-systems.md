## Summary

Introduction to Machine Learning Systems: Principles and Practices of Engineering Artificially Intelligent Systems is a textbook authored by Prof. Vijay Janapa Reddi of Harvard University's School of Engineering and Applied Sciences, dated September 4, 2025 (as printed in the converted markdown). The book covers the engineering principles of ML systems from foundations (AI pervasiveness, AI/ML basics, historical eras of AI, ML lifecycle, DL primer, DNN architectures) through principles (AI workflow, deployment stages, maintenance) and across deployment contexts (cloud, edge, mobile, TinyML, hybrid). It includes a featured AI learning companion called SocratiQ AI, is openly licensed, and was developed with community collaboration and support from funding agencies, academic institutions, non-profits, and corporate sponsors.

## Key Claims

- The book is authored by Prof. Vijay Janapa Reddi at Harvard SEAS, with the markdown conversion carrying a September 4, 2025 date.
- AI evolution is structured into five eras: Symbolic AI, Expert Systems, Statistical Learning, Shallow Learning, and Deep Learning.
- ML systems are categorised by deployment context into Cloud ML, Edge ML, Mobile ML, TinyML, and Hybrid ML, each with distinct characteristics, benefits, challenges, and use cases.
- The book includes a SocratiQ AI learning companion.
- Practical application case studies covered include FarmBeats (agriculture), AlphaFold (scientific ML), and Autonomous Vehicles.
- The USPS Postal Service is used as a deep-learning case study in the DL Primer chapter.
- The book is modular in design with suggested reading paths and is developed with community collaboration under an open copyright/licensing model.
- Challenges in ML systems are grouped into data-related, model-related, system-related, and ethical considerations.
