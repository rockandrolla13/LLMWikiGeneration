# Economics for Investment Decision Makers Workbook: Micro, Macro, and International Economics

**Authors:** Christopher D. Piros, Jerald E. Pinto · **Year:** 2013 · **Venue:** John Wiley & Sons (CFA Institute Investment Series) · **Type:** book

## Summary

The companion Workbook to the CFA Institute Investment Series text 'Economics for Investment Decision Makers', providing Learning Outcome Statements, summaries, and practice problems across twelve chapters spanning microeconomics (demand/supply, the firm, market structures), macroeconomics (aggregate output, business cycles, monetary and fiscal policy), and international economics (trade, capital flows, exchange-rate determination, economic growth). It reinforces the core economic frameworks an analyst uses to interpret macro conditions, policy regimes, and currency dynamics when making investment decisions.

## Key Claims

1. The book is organised into 12 chapters across micro, macro, and international economics, as Learning Outcomes, Summaries, and Practice Problems with Solutions.
2. Microeconomic demand/supply analysis covers price, income, and cross-price elasticity and consumer/producer surplus.
3. Firm and market-structure analysis distinguishes perfect competition, monopolistic competition, oligopoly, and monopoly.
4. Macroeconomic coverage links aggregate output and prices to growth, business-cycle phases, and monetary and fiscal policy.
5. International coverage treats trade and capital flows, currency exchange-rate determination and forecasting, and growth in the investment decision.

## Questions Raised

- How do exchange-rate determination and forecasting frameworks compare to modern empirical FX models?
- How are monetary and fiscal policy interactions framed relative to post-2008 unconventional policy?
