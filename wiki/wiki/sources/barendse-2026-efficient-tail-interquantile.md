# Efficiently Weighted Estimation of Tail and Interquantile Expectations

**Authors:** [[entities/sander-barendse|Sander Barendse]]

**Venue:** *Journal of Financial Econometrics*, 24(2), nbag003 (2026)

**DOI:** [10.1093/jjfinec/nbag003](https://doi.org/10.1093/jjfinec/nbag003)

## Summary

Develops a [[concepts/semiparametric-estimation|semiparametric]] two-stage joint estimator for left- and right-[[concepts/tail-expectation|tail expectations]] together with [[concepts/interquantile-expectation|interquantile expectations]] between two thresholding quantiles in nonlinear, time-series settings. Derives efficient weights from the [[concepts/fissler-ziegel-loss|Fissler-Ziegel]] class, asymptotics under beta-mixing, and applies the framework to test for disproportionate tail-event contributions to [[concepts/fama-french-factors|Fama-French]] abnormal returns of size and momentum portfolios.

## Key Claims

1. The proposed weighted estimator is locally robust to first-stage quantile estimation and attains efficiency within the Fissler-Ziegel class
2. Efficient weighting reduces parameter standard deviations and improves CI coverage in simulations
3. Small-market-equity stocks show a disproportionately large positive tail-event contribution to the average abnormal return
