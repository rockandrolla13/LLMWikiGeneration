# US Interest Rate Strategist: Stay Close to Home

**Authors:** Jim Caron · **Year:** 2009 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's US Interest Rate Strategist note (November 12, 2009) by Jim Caron, head of Global Interest Rate Strategy. Despite record Treasury issuance, the yield curve and rates traded in a tight range amid dovish Fed speak, and the team expects reduced risk appetite into year-end. The core longer-term view is for the curve to bear-steepen with rising rates, driven by supply, prolonged easy policy, and rising inflation risk premiums, while near-term bond fund inflows may keep yields low. The note details tactical trades including a 2s10s flattener, swaption calendar spreads, and rate caps.

## Key Claims

1. Longer term, rates are expected to rise led by the back end of the curve, driven by supply, easy policy, and rising inflation risk premiums.
2. Near-term, a record pace of inflows into bond funds from money market funds may keep yields low and the curve flat.
3. The team is biased for credit/agency spreads to widen long term but expects them to stay range-bound at tight levels near term.
4. Volatility is expected to remain directional with rates; the team stays long volatility on longer tails and long rate caps.
