# The Global Macro Analyst: Japanification or Salvation

**Authors:** Joachim Fels, Manoj Pradhan, Patryk Drozdzik, Sung Woen Kang, Philipp Erfurth · **Year:** 2013 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's Global Macro Analyst (October 30, 2013) warns that the euro area risks falling into a deflationary 'Japanification' trap analogous to Japan's post-bubble experience of the 1990s and 2000s. Joachim Fels argues this can be avoided if policymakers heed three lessons: move monetary policy early and aggressively before deflation sets in, enforce a clean-up and recapitalisation of bank balance sheets, and avoid premature fiscal tightening. A spotlight section examines why the FOMC surprised markets by not tapering asset purchases in September, attributing it to the heavy weight of mortgage rates in the Fed's financial-conditions assessment.

## Key Claims

1. The euro area faces a serious risk of 'Japanification' — a prolonged deflationary balance-sheet recession mirroring Japan's experience after its bubble burst.
2. Japanification can likely be avoided if euro area policymakers move monetary policy early, clean up bank balance sheets, and avoid premature fiscal tightening.
3. The Fed's September 2013 decision not to taper was driven by a 'rapid tightening of financial conditions', with mortgage rates carrying disproportionate weight in macro models.
4. Global GDP growth is forecast at 2.9% (2013E) rising to 3.5%-3.7% in 2014-2015, with emerging markets outpacing G10 economies.
