# Treasury Market Commentary, January 25

**Authors:** Tony Small, Matthew Hornbach, Robert Rosener, Molly Wharton, Guneet Dhingra, Sam Elprince, David Harris · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

This daily US rates strategy commentary recaps a broad risk-on session driven by a WSJ report that the Fed may end balance-sheet normalization earlier, a deal to reopen the US government, and a dovish ECB. Equities, CDX IG/HY and oil rallied while G4 yields rose and European periphery spreads tightened, with the US 10y closing up 4.3bp at 2.758%. A notable theme was broad-based flattening of 5s30s curves across European semi-core and periphery markets to multi-year flats. The note previews next week's FOMC meeting, key US data and coupon auctions.

## Key Claims

1. A WSJ report that the Fed may end balance-sheet normalization earlier helped drive a broad risk-on session, easing perceived quantitative tightening pressure.
2. European semi-core and periphery 5s30s curves flattened broadly to their flattest levels in years, with Spain outperforming.
3. The US 10y Treasury yield rose 4.3bp to 2.758% and the 30y outperformed, with curves flattening modestly.
4. Market focus next week is on FOMC balance-sheet discussion, major US data, and back-to-back nominal coupon supply for the first time since December 2013.
