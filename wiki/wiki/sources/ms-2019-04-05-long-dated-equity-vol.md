# Long-Dated Vol Lines Up

**Authors:** Phanikiran L Naraparaju, Andrew Sheets, Serena W Tang, Wanting Low, Naomi Z Poole · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A cross-asset strategy note arguing that long-dated equity options are most attractive when volatility is low, equity carry is high, and markets are inexpensive, a confluence present in the UK, eurozone and Japan. Because options price off a forward point driven by dividend yield less the risk-free rate, multi-year-low rates relative to dividend yields make options to buy markets at current levels in five years unusually cheap. The authors prefer 5-year ATMF calls on the FTSE 100 as a defensive way to express positive views on underowned, high-carry markets. The dynamic is judged less compelling for US and EM equities.

## Key Claims

1. Long-dated equity options are cheapest when low volatility, high equity carry, and attractive valuations coincide, which currently holds for UK, European and Japanese equities.
2. With rates at multi-year lows and below dividend yields, equity forwards trade at a discount to spot, making 5-year calls to buy at current levels historically cheap.
3. 5-year ATMF calls on the FTSE 100 are the preferred trade, profitable as long as the index is at or above current levels in five years.
