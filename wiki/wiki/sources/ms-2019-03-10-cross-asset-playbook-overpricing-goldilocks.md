# Cross-Asset Playbook – March 2019: Overpricing Goldilocks, Underpricing Tails

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Wanting Low, Naomi Poole, Serena Tang · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's March 2019 cross-asset note argues the market is too confident in a 'Goldilocks' central scenario of stable growth, absent inflation and on-hold G3 policy, while underpricing tail outcomes such as a stronger China-led growth rebound or a sharper hit from expected 1Q earnings weakness. In either tail, the team expects US and rest-of-world growth to converge. They remain unenthusiastic on market beta but optimistic on turning points in five key relationships, holding equal-weight equities and government bonds, underweight credit and overweight cash. New trades include adding Mexico local bonds (Mbonos '29s, FX-hedged), closing Stoxx Miners in favour of an FTSE 100 long, and buying CDX IG puts.

## Key Claims

1. The market overprices a Goldilocks central scenario and underprices both upside and downside tails.
2. In either tail, US and rest-of-world growth are expected to converge.
3. Five key relationships offer attractive asymmetry: Broad USD, US vs. RoW equities, US vs. European duration, EM vs. corporate credit, and value vs. growth.
4. Allocation remains equal-weight equities and government bonds, underweight credit, overweight cash.
