# European Credit Strategy — What We're Watching

**Authors:** Srikanth Sankaran, Max Blass, Aron Becker · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A weekly European credit strategy data note from Morgan Stanley. The firm's economists expect euro area 4Q GDP releases to show continued softness driven partly by transitory factors such as French protests and weakness in the German car industry, with Brexit votes also in focus. Credit performance was strong but characterised by a decompression trend, as EUR IG spreads tightened 3bp while HY spreads widened 3bp. The note argues the rally is 'not a beta-driven rally', with better-quality and shorter-duration credit outperforming.

## Key Claims

1. Euro area 4Q GDP softness is partly driven by transitory factors such as the French protests and German auto weakness.
2. Credit performance showed a decompression trend, with EUR IG tightening 3bp and HY widening 3bp, and better-quality credit outperforming.
3. The recent credit rally is not beta-driven, as evidenced by the widening B-BB basis and underperformance of corporate hybrids.
