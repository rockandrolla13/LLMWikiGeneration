# Mixed-Frequency Macro-Finance Factor Models: Theory and Applications

**Authors:** [[entities/elena-andreou|Elena Andreou]], [[entities/patrick-gagliardini|Patrick Gagliardini]], [[entities/eric-ghysels|Eric Ghysels]], [[entities/mirco-rubin|Mirco Rubin]]

**Venue:** *Journal of Financial Econometrics*, 18(3), 585-628 (2020)

**DOI:** [10.1093/jjfinec/nbaa015](https://doi.org/10.1093/jjfinec/nbaa015)

## Summary

Develops asymptotic theory for testing common factors across two large panels of macroeconomic and financial variables sampled at different frequencies, comparing aggregation-first vs PCA-first procedures within a [[concepts/group-factor-models|group factor model]] framework that uses [[concepts/midas-regression|MIDAS regression]] for [[concepts/mixed-frequency-data|mixed-frequency data]]. Empirically finds no single common macro-finance factor in 1963-2017 US data overall, but evidence of one common factor in pre- and post-[[concepts/great-moderation|Great Moderation]] subperiods, with structural changes in loadings.

## Key Claims

1. Aggregation-first and PCA-first procedures yield nearly identical common-factor estimates under mild conditions
2. There is evidence of a single macro-finance common factor in pre- and post-Great Moderation regimes but not the full 1963-2017 sample
3. Common and group-specific factors significantly predict real GDP, VIX, VRP, corporate spreads, and ETF returns
