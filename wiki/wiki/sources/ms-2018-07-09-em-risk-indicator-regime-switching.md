# EM Quant Strategy: EM Risk Indicator - A Regime-Switching Model Approach

**Authors:** Andres Jaime · **Year:** 2018 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley introduces an EM regime-switching model that acts as an early indicator to pre-empt stress in EM assets, classifying the environment into risk-seeking, neutral and risk-averse regimes using inputs such as term premia, the TED spread, IG/HY credit spreads, rates vol, currency vol and the VIX. EM return distributions differ materially across regimes, with volatility, skew, kurtosis and drawdowns rising as the model moves away from risk-seeking. Regime shifts have served as high-hit-ratio trading signals in-sample, especially when shifting from extremes and when persistent for more than a month. The model accurately flagged a 2018 shift from risk-seeking to neutral, after which GBI-EM lost 10% and EMBI+ spreads widened 70bp.

## Key Claims

1. The EM regime-switching model classifies markets into risk-seeking, neutral and risk-averse regimes with thresholds at 22% and 55%.
2. EM return distributions (volatility, skew, kurtosis, drawdowns) differ materially across regimes, with implications for asset allocation.
3. Regime shifts are good trading signals, particularly when moving from extremes and persisting beyond a month.
4. The model signalled an early-2018 shift to neutral, after which GBI-EM fell 10% and EMBI+ spreads widened 70bp.
