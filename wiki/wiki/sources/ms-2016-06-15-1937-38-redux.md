# Global Macro Briefing: 1937-38 Redux?

**Authors:** Chetan Ahya, Elga Bartsch, Jonathan Ashworth · **Year:** 2016 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley draws parallels between the current macro environment and the 1930s, arguing that high indebtedness and a financial shock changed private-sector risk attitudes and triggered balance-sheet repair, keeping demand and inflation expectations subdued. As monetary policy approaches its limits, the firm contends fiscal policy must be activated, but hesitant or politically constrained policymakers keep alive the risk of a 1937-38-style relapse into recession. The base case is a hesitant policy response and below-trend global growth, framed around a structural '3D' challenge of debt, demographics, and disinflation.

## Key Claims

1. The current cycle resembles the 1930s because high indebtedness and a financial shock drove private-sector deleveraging, subduing demand and inflation expectations.
2. With monetary policy near its limits, fiscal stimulus is needed to avoid a 1937-38-style relapse into recession, but policymakers remain hesitant or constrained.
3. A structural '3D' challenge of debt, demographics, and disinflation explains persistently below-trend global growth.
