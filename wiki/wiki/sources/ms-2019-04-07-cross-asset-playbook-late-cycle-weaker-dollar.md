# Cross-Asset Playbook – April 2019: Late-Cycle and Weaker Dollar

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Wanting Low, Naomi Poole, Serena Tang · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

The April 2019 Cross-Asset Strategy Playbook positions for a late-cycle US market and a weaker dollar. The team stays equal-weight equities, underweight credit, equal-weight government bonds and overweight cash, preferring ex-US assets (EM and Japan equities, EM sovereign credit) where growth bull cases and higher risk premiums exist. It flags a large gap between improved market prices and modest data, raising the 'pressure to deliver' for 1Q earnings, especially in the US. Trades concentrate defensive hedges in the US while placing beta outside it, including Indonesia and Mexico local bond positions and breakeven inflation cross-market trades.

## Key Claims

1. The team is positioned for late-cycle US dynamics and a weaker dollar: EW equities, UW credit, EW government bonds, OW cash.
2. EM and Japan are the most preferred equity regions and the US the least favoured; EM sovereign credit is preferred over DM corporate and securitized.
3. A gap between improved prices and modest data increases the 'pressure to deliver', with US 1Q earnings expected to disappoint.
4. Hedges are US-centric while beta is concentrated outside the US, via Indonesia and Mexico local positions and breakeven cross-market trades.
