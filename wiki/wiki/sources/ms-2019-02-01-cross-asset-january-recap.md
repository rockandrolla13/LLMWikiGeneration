# Cross-Asset Strategy: Global In the Flow — Highlights from January

**Authors:** Wanting Low, Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Serena Tang, Naomi Poole · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A cross-asset strategy recap of January 2019, when stocks and credit rebounded sharply after a tough year-end driven by a dovish Fed pivot. EM led with a 9% return, followed by the US (+8%), Europe (+6%), and Japan (+5%), with US high yield posting the largest reversal at 408bp excess returns. The note covers performance, technicals (negative net issuance in DM IG, HY, and EM), and moderating sentiment, while flagging the risk of an earnings recession.

## Key Claims

1. EM equities led January with a 9% return, ahead of the US (+8%), Europe (+6%), and Japan (+5%).
2. US high yield saw the largest reversal, posting 408bp of excess returns in January.
3. DM IG, HY, and EM credit net issuance turned negative year on year while DM sovereign net issuance stayed high.
4. Sentiment moderated after December's fear, with the VIX falling to ~17% and the MS Global Risk Demand Index at 1.6.
