# Cross-Asset Brief: Taking Stock — Recent Moves in Context

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran L Naraparaju, Serena W Tang, Wanting Low, Naomi Z Poole · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Following the worst one-day return for global stocks since 2008, Morgan Stanley provides a cross-asset snapshot of price and valuation data across equities, credit, rates, FX, commodities and volatility. They note that lower bond yields and weaker risk assets are pushing risk premiums up, with equity risk premiums near 10-year extremes across most markets while credit risk premiums remained relatively low. Volatility across all asset classes moved into the top decile of its 15-year history, with options-implied daily moves of ~2.5% for the S&P 500 and 7bp for CDX IG. Term structure and skew measures were as extreme as 2008 or worse, and 2020 had already matched all of 2019's 3-sigma cross-asset moves by March.

## Key Claims

1. Equity risk premiums sit near 10-year extremes across most markets while credit risk premiums remain relatively low.
2. Cross-asset volatility entered the top decile of its 15-year history, with term structure and skew as extreme as 2008.
3. 2020 had already seen as many 3-sigma cross-asset moves by March as all of 2019.

## Questions Raised

- Why do credit risk premiums lag equity risk premiums in repricing during the selloff?
- Will extreme volatility term structure and skew normalize or persist?
