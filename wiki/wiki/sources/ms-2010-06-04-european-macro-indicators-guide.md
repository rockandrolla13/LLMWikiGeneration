# European Economics: A Practitioner's Guide to European Macro Indicators

**Authors:** Elga Bartsch, Daniele Antonucci, Melanie Baker, Cath Sleeman · **Year:** 2010 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

The fourth edition of Morgan Stanley's compendium of important European economic indicators, compiled in response to investor questions about their relevance for the business cycle and monetary policy. It provides a list of top indicators for each region chosen for their potential to move markets and to change the firm's assessment of the business cycle and interest rate outlook. New features include an overview of European institutions and rules of thumb on the impact of external factors on euro area growth and inflation. National data are highlighted as still relevant because they are often released earlier than aggregate Euroland data.

## Key Claims

1. The top indicators to watch are selected for their potential to move markets and shift the assessment of the business cycle and interest rate outlook.
2. National data remain worth watching in the EMU world because they are often released earlier than aggregate Euroland data.
3. The top-10 European indicators span Germany (Ifo, regional CPI, labour market), EMU (PMI, HICP flash, M3), France (INSEE, consumption) and the UK (CPI, average earnings/claimant count).
