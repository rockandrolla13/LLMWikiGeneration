# Economic Analysis Through Mathematics: Tools and Techniques for Decision Making

**Authors:** Zrinka Lukač · **Year:** 2026 · **Venue:** Springer Texts in Business and Economics · **Type:** book

## Summary

A textbook (Springer, 2026) by Zrinka Lukač of the University of Zagreb. The central thesis is that economic phenomena - production, optimal allocation of scarce resources, pricing, financial decisions - are best understood through systematic mathematical formalization. It is organized into five parts: linear algebra, single-variable differential calculus, multivariable calculus, integrals/differential equations, and mathematics of finance. Each tool is paired with an economic application (input-output analysis, marginal analysis, elasticity, Lagrange-multiplier optimization, amortization).

## Key Claims

1. Matrices and linear algebra provide the foundational framework for organizing economic data and solving structural models.
2. Systems of linear equations underpin input-output (Leontief) analysis and equilibrium models.
3. Differential calculus formalizes marginal analysis, with optimization via first- and second-derivative conditions.
4. Elasticity is expressed via derivatives and homogeneous production functions characterized via Euler's theorem.
5. Constrained economic optimization is solved by substitution or Lagrange multipliers.
6. Mathematics of finance (compound/continuous interest, discounting, annuities, amortization) provides the basis for financial decision making.

## Questions Raised

- How do closed-form optimization techniques scale to high-dimensional or data-driven economic problems?
- Where does deterministic modeling break down relative to stochastic/empirical methods?
