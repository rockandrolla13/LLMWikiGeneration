# Global In the Flow First Quarter Recap: What a Year This Quarter Has Been

**Authors:** Wanting Low, Naomi Poole, Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Serena Tang · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's cross-asset 'In the Flow' first-quarter 2020 recap chronicles the COVID-19 market shock, noting 1Q20 saw more extreme 3-sigma moves than the whole of 2008. The S&P 500 posted its worst quarter since 4Q08 and worst-ever first quarter, US HY spreads saw their largest widening since 4Q08, and the VIX hit a record 83% in mid-March. Government bonds rallied with UST 10Y yields falling the most since 2008, the trade-weighted USD strengthened against most G10 and EM currencies except safe havens, and Brent fell 64%. DM investment-grade gross issuance picked up to a five-year high while HY and EM issuance dipped.

## Key Claims

1. 1Q20 saw more extreme 3-sigma cross-asset moves than the whole of 2008, marking the start (and in some markets the trough) of a bear market.
2. The S&P 500 had its worst quarter since 4Q08 and worst first quarter ever, while US HY spreads widened the most since 4Q08 and US IG spreads widened more than in 2008.
3. Equity volatility spiked with the VIX reaching a record 83% in mid-March, and Brent crude fell 64% on the quarter as the hardest-hit commodity.
4. DM investment-grade gross issuance reached its strongest run rate in five years even as DM high-yield and EM issuance dipped.
