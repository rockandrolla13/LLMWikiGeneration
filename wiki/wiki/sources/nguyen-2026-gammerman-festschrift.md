# The Importance of Being Learnable: Essays Dedicated to Alexander Gammerman

**Editors:** Khuong An Nguyen, Zhiyuan Luo (both Royal Holloway, University of London)

**Year:** 2026

**Venue:** Springer, *Lecture Notes in Computer Science* 16290. ISBN 978-3-032-15120-9 (eBook). 478 pages.

## Summary

A **Festschrift** for [[entities/alexander-gammerman|Alexander Gammerman]] — co-inventor of [[concepts/conformal-prediction|conformal prediction]] with [[entities/vladimir-vovk|Vovk]] and [[entities/glenn-shafer|Shafer]], and longtime head of the Computer Learning Research Centre at Royal Holloway. The volume collects essays from collaborators, students, and successors across the CP, machine learning, and statistical learning communities.

A 478-page edited collection of this nature contains many independent contributions. This page indexes the volume as a single source; specific chapters can be promoted to their own source pages when individual essays prove load-bearing.

## Why This Source Matters

Gammerman's role in CP is sometimes under-recognised compared to Vovk and Shafer (whose names appear on the foundational textbook [[sources/vovk-2005-algorithmic-learning|Vovk-Gammerman-Shafer 2005]]). This Festschrift consolidates a generation of CP work that emanated from Royal Holloway — papers on Venn predictors, on-line compression models, conformal predictive distributions, and applications to drug design, bioinformatics, and finance. Worth treating as a **survey-and-anchor source** for Royal Holloway-lineage CP work.
