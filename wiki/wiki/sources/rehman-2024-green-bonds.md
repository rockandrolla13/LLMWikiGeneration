# Unraveling the Multiscale Comovement of Green Bonds and Structural Shocks

## Summary

Examines multiscale comovement between green bonds in developed countries and international oil-driven shocks using wavelet techniques. Uses structural VAR to decompose oil shocks into demand, supply, and risk components. Finds limited correlation overall, with Norway and Sweden showing stronger connections at low frequencies.

## Key Findings

- **Limited Correlation**: Generally weak connection between green bonds and oil markets
- **Norway/Sweden Exception**: Strong comovement at low frequencies
- **Crisis-Dependent**: Correlation varies during turbulent periods
- **Risk Shock Sensitivity**: Norway and Sweden susceptible to risk shocks at low frequencies

## Methodology

### Oil Shock Decomposition (Ready 2018)
- Demand shocks
- Supply shocks
- Risk shocks (using VIX)
- Global oil and gas returns (not just WTI)

### Wavelet Analysis
- Wavelet coherence and cross-wavelet analysis
- Partial and multiple wavelet correlations
- Time-frequency decomposition (short vs long-term)
- Wavelet VaR for risk analysis

## Countries Analyzed

UK, US, Japan, Canada, Australia, Europe (including Norway, Sweden)

Data: November 2008 - June 2021

## Key Results

| Market | Correlation with Oil | Risk Reduction Ability |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Most markets | Limited (low scales) | Low |
| Norway | Strong (low freq) | Low |
| Sweden | Strong (low freq) | Low |

## Implications for Investors

1. Green bonds offer diversification from oil shocks (most markets)
2. Norway/Sweden green bonds less effective as oil hedges
3. Hedging effectiveness varies with time horizon
4. Crisis periods show different correlation patterns
