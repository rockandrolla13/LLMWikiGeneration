# MREL – Raising the Subordination Stakes

**Authors:** Jackie Ineke, Joe Hopkins, Alvaro Serrano, Izabel Dobreva · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A European bank credit and equity note assessing the impact of the finalised BRRD2 and tougher SRB policy on eurozone bank MREL issuance needs. The BRRD2 introduces a non-risk-weighted backstop of 8% of Total Liabilities and Own Funds (reducible to ~6.6%), supplementing the existing RWA-based requirement of roughly 26% on average. The SRB raised minimum subordination targets for GSIIs and OSIIs to 16% and 14% plus the combined buffer ratio. Re-running issuance estimates, the authors project eurozone non-preferred senior issuance needs of roughly EUR 243bn to EUR 363bn, with the bulk expected over the next three years ahead of the January 2024 deadline.

## Key Claims

1. BRRD2 adds an 8% TLOF non-risk-weighted backstop measure to MREL, with a bank's requirement set at the higher of the TLOF and RWA measures.
2. The SRB raised minimum subordination targets for GSIIs to 16% and OSIIs to 14% plus the combined buffer ratio, up from 13.5% and 12%.
3. Eurozone bank MREL-related issuance needs are estimated at roughly EUR 243bn to EUR 363bn, expected mostly over the next three years.
