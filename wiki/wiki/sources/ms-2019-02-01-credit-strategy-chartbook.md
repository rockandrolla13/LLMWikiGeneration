# US Corporate Credit Strategy Chartbook

**Authors:** Adam S Richmond, Vishwas Patkar, Frederick T Fuchs, Felician G Stratmann, Jingli Li · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

This US Corporate Credit Strategy Chartbook compiles cross-asset and credit market data as of early February 2019, recapping 2019 year-to-date total and excess returns by quality bucket and asset class. It covers IG and HY valuations by rating and sector, curve performance, new issuance, liquidity metrics and fundamentals, plus derivatives and structured credit including cash-CDS basis. The market summary shows a broad risk-on start to 2019 with credit spreads sharply tighter, equities up strongly, and government bond yields lower. It serves as a reference data pack rather than a thesis-driven note.

## Key Claims

1. 2019 year-to-date returns were strongly positive across credit quality buckets and risk assets, with HY corporate spreads ~99bp tighter on the year.
2. US IG and HY spreads tightened materially over one and three months alongside an equity rally and lower government yields.
3. The chartbook tracks valuations by rating and sector, issuance, liquidity, fundamentals and derivatives/structured credit metrics.
4. Cash-CDS basis and relative value across US vs Europe and IG vs HY are monitored in the derivatives section.
