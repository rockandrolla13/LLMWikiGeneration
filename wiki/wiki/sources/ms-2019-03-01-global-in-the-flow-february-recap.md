# Global In the Flow: February Recap

**Authors:** Wanting Low, Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Naomi Poole, Serena Tang · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's March 1, 2019 'Global In the Flow' note recaps a strong February in which risk assets continued rallying. The S&P 500, up 11.5% year-to-date, posted its best start to a year since 1991, and US high yield (up 5.9% in excess returns YTD) had its best start since 2001, with Chinese equities up 14% in USD. Technicals show weak gross issuance in DM HY (-22% YTD) and EM (-24%), feeding declining net issuance, while DM sovereign net issuance stayed in an uptrend. Sentiment turned more confident: VIX fell to 14.8% and the MS Global Risk Demand Index rose to 1.4.

## Key Claims

1. S&P 500 up 11.5% YTD had its best first-two-months since 1991; US HY had its best start since 2001.
2. Chinese equities rose 14% in USD terms during February.
3. DM HY (-22%) and EM (-24%) YTD gross issuance fell year-on-year, while DM sovereign net issuance kept rising.
4. Sentiment turned more confident with VIX at 14.8% and the MS Global Risk Demand Index at 1.4.
