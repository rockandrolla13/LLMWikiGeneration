# Goldilocks Whiplash (Sunday Start: What's Next in Global Macro)

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

In this Sunday Start macro note, Chief Cross-Asset Strategist Andrew Sheets argues markets have swung from December recession fears to a 'Goldilocks' narrative of low inflation, patient central banks, and resolving US political risks, and expresses scepticism that the story holds together. He notes DM economies have less spare capacity than in prior Goldilocks periods given near-50-year-low unemployment and rising wage growth, while global growth data (trade, PMIs, earnings revisions) remain weak. Morgan Stanley favours being short the broad USD and overweight international over US equities, and views US real rates as too high.

## Key Claims

1. The Goldilocks narrative depends on a lack of inflation, but DM economies have significantly less spare capacity than in prior Goldilocks periods (2010-16).
2. Global growth data remain poor, with broad-based weakness in trade data, global PMIs, and earnings revisions over the prior three months.
3. Morgan Stanley favours being short the broad USD and overweight international over US equities, and judges US 10-year real rates too high relative to a Fed that may be done hiking.
