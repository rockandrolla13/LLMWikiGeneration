# Global Insights Day 2019 - Polling Results

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Chetan Ahya, Hans W Redeker, Jonathan F Garner, Graham Secker, Neil McLeish, Jeremy J Nalewaik, Matthew Hornbach, Adam S Richmond, Michael J Wilson, Michael D Zezas, Vishwanath Tirupattur, Chiente Hsu · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A Global Cross-Asset Strategy note summarizing informal investor polling at Morgan Stanley's New York Global Insights conference on March 1, 2019. Results show moderate caution on risk exposure, bullishness on the S&P 500, aversion to UK and Japanese equities, a technology sector preference, and expectations for higher 2019 Treasury yields above strategists' base case. Investors also anticipate wider US IG credit spreads and largely position neutral-to-underweight duration, with most expecting 2s10s yield-curve inversion before the next recession.

## Key Claims

1. 78% of NY investors expect the S&P 500 to end 2019 at 2800 or above, more bullish than Morgan Stanley strategists' 2500-2800 fair-value range.
2. 67% of investors expect Treasury yields to end 2019 higher, versus strategists' 2.45% 10y UST base case.
3. 70% of clients expect US IG credit spreads to widen through end-2019, agreeing with the firm's credit strategists.
4. 75% expect the 2s10s curve to invert before the next recession, and 95% see a recession within two years of any inversion.
