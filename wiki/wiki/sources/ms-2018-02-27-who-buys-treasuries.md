# US Interest Rate Strategy: Who Is Going to Buy Treasuries?

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach · **Year:** 2018 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley argues that foreign official buyers have resumed purchasing US Treasuries after a brief year-end hiatus, with NY Fed custody holdings up $58bn over four weeks since January 24, 2018, the largest such increase since June 2017. Contrary to the popular narrative of foreign selling, foreign investors actually net bought $20bn of Treasuries in 2017, versus $325bn of net sales in 2016. The note contends higher long-end yields will attract enough foreign and domestic demand to clear coming supply, and reiterates a preference for UST 2s30s curve flatteners.

## Key Claims

1. NY Fed custody holdings of Treasuries rose $58bn in the four weeks since January 24, 2018, the largest four-week increase since June 2017.
2. Foreign investors net bought $20bn of Treasuries in 2017, contrasting with $325bn of net sales in 2016.
3. Foreign ownership of Treasuries remains near 40% of outstanding, though foreign official share of foreign holdings fell from 75% to 64% since the crisis.
4. Higher available yields should attract sufficient demand to clear increased Treasury supply; the team likes UST 2s30s curve flatteners.
