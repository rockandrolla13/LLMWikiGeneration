# European Banks: Hunting for Value in Tier 2 and Seniors

**Authors:** Joe Hopkins, Jackie Ineke · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley scrubs European bank Tier 2, non-preferred senior (NPS) and preferred senior bonds for relative value, noting NPS spreads have yet to find a stable level between Tier 2 and preferred senior, with relationships varying by bank and geography. They see most value in French NPS (French Tier 2 trades rich versus NPS), Spanish Tier 2 and preferred (barbelling Santander/BBVA), and German preferred (German NPS trades rich). The team recommends buying selected BPCE, SocGen, Santander and Commerzbank bonds and selling others, while noting that in resolution both Tier 2 and NPS would be written down to zero, so they generally prefer Tier 2 for the extra yield.

## Key Claims

1. Non-preferred senior spreads have not stabilised between Tier 2 and preferred senior, with the most value seen in French NPS, Spanish Tier 2/preferred and German preferred.
2. French Tier 2 trades rich to NPS versus the sector average, so the most outright value is in NPS, favouring low-net-issuance issuers SocGen and BPCE.
3. Specific buys include BPCE 1.375% 26 and SocGen 1.125% 25 NPS, Santander 2.5% 25 Tier 2, and Santander/Commerzbank preferred; sells include BPCE/SocGen Tier 2 and BBVA preferred.
4. In a resolution scenario both Tier 2 and NPS are expected to be written down to zero, so the team generally prefers Tier 2 for the extra yield.
