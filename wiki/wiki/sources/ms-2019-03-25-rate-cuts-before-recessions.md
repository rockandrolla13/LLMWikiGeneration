# US Interest Rate Strategist: What Also Comes Before Recessions? Rate Cuts

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Following the March 22, 2019 inversion of the 3m/10y Treasury yield spread (the first since 2007), Morgan Stanley argues that Fed rate cuts have been as reliable a recession predictor as the yield curve. Studying the seven NBER recessions since the late 1960s, the note finds the 3m/10y curve was inverted immediately before the first four but not the last three, a difference attributed to the Fed switching to federal funds rate targeting in October 1982. Since then the Fed has cut rates on average 169 business days (about eight months) after the curve first inverted, implying a potential cut around December 2019. The team maintains UST 2s5s flatteners and long 5y TIPS positions.

## Key Claims

1. On March 22, 2019 the 3m/10y Treasury spread inverted for the first time since August 27, 2007.
2. The 3m/10y curve was inverted right before the first four NBER recessions but not the last three (1990, 2001, 2007).
3. Since adopting funds-rate targeting in October 1982, the Fed has cut rates on average 169 business days after the first 3m/10y inversion.
4. A rate cut in line with that average would arrive around December 2019; the team suggests UST 2s5s flatteners and long 5y TIPS.
