# Europe looks set to surprise on the upside

**Authors:** Graham Secker, Ross A MacDonald, Daniele Antonucci, Srikanth Sankaran, Hans W Redeker, Gek Teng Khoo, Tony Small, David S. Adams · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley argues European macro newsflow is set to improve as 2H18 idiosyncratic drags fade, China recovers, wage growth accelerates and fiscal stimulus picks up, with the euro area composite PMI judged to have troughed in January 2019. This is seen as positive for European equities and the euro, supporting a buy of China exposure, UK exposure, 'cyclical cyclicals' and an upgrade of Real Estate to Overweight while staying Neutral Banks. In fixed income the team is underweight European government bonds versus North America, expects higher Bund yields and a stronger EUR (targeting 1.18 in EURUSD by summer), and adds China exposure in EUR IG via Autos and Industrials.

## Key Claims

1. European macro newsflow should improve from here as idiosyncratic 2H18 drags fade, China rebounds, wages accelerate and fiscal stimulus picks up; euro area composite PMI is judged to have troughed in January 2019.
2. MSCI Europe's risk-reward improves materially after a PMI trough and correlates positively with China equities, supporting buying China/UK exposure and 'cyclical cyclicals' and upgrading Real Estate to Overweight.
3. The team is underweight European government bonds versus North America and expects higher Bund yields and a stronger EUR, using 1.12 EURUSD as a buying opportunity targeting 1.18 by summer.
4. Within EUR credit they add China exposure via Autos and Industrials and are neutral Financials after their rally.
