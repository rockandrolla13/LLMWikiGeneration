# UK Interest Rate Strategist: Gilt Futures: The Return of the Option

**Authors:** Mayank Gargh, Laurence Mutkin, Owen Roberts, Michelle Bradley · **Year:** 2008 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A UK Interest Rate Strategist note arguing that the December Gilt futures contract embeds meaningful cheapest-to-deliver optionality because two issues, the 2018 and 2021 gilts, each have roughly a 50% probability of becoming the final CTD. The authors believe the market underestimates this optionality and recommend buying the December Gilt basis with a 50% weight on the 2018 and 50% on the 2021 as a cheap way to be long gamma. The large dv01 difference between the front and back ends of the delivery basket has implications for trading and hedging in the December and subsequent March 2009 contracts.

## Key Claims

1. The December Gilt contract has two issues vying to be CTD, each with roughly 50% probability, unlike the September contract.
2. The market is underestimating the optionality of a CTD switch, making buying the Dec Gilt basis a cheap way to be long gamma.
3. The significant dv01 difference between the front and back of the delivery basket has important trading and hedging implications.
