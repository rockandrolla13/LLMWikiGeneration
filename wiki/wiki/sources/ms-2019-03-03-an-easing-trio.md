# An Easing Trio (Sunday Start: What's Next in Global Macro)

**Authors:** Chetan Ahya · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Chief Economist Chetan Ahya argues that global growth will bottom in 1Q19, supported by an 'easing trio': easing trade tensions, China's monetary easing starting to work, and the Fed's flexibility and patience easing global financial conditions. He recounts how the same three factors caused growth outside the US to slow from 4.2%Y in 1Q18 to 3.4%Y in 4Q18, with business uncertainty and tighter financial conditions the key transmission channels. Morgan Stanley is increasingly confident 1Q19 marks the trough, with the recovery centred outside the US and in emerging markets.

## Key Claims

1. An 'easing trio' (easing trade tensions, China monetary easing, and Fed flexibility) supports the view that 1Q19 marks the trough of the global growth mini-cycle.
2. Growth outside the US slowed from a peak of 4.2%Y in 1Q18 to 3.4%Y in 4Q18, transmitted via heightened business uncertainty and tighter financial conditions rather than direct tariff effects.
3. The recovery should be concentrated outside the US and in emerging markets in particular.
