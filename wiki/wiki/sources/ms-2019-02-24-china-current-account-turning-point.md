# Another Turning Point (Sunday Start: What's Next in Global Macro)

**Authors:** James K Lord · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Head of EM Fixed Income Strategy James Lord argues that China's economy should slip into its first annual current account deficit since 1993, a structural shift driven by an aging population that will consume more and save less. The deficit is forecast at US$50 billion in 2019 and as much as US$430 billion by 2030, requiring a significant ramp-up in capital inflows. With foreign ownership of Chinese government bonds, equities, and corporate bonds very low, index inclusion (Bloomberg Global Aggregate from April, FTSE Russell, MSCI) should drive large inflows into Chinese fixed income and equities.

## Key Claims

1. China should record its first annual current account deficit since 1993, a structural shift driven by an aging population, reaching US$50bn in 2019 and up to US$430bn by 2030.
2. Foreign ownership of Chinese assets is tiny (8% of government bonds, 2.6% of equities, under 2% of corporate bonds) and set to rise sharply on index inclusion.
3. Chinese government bond inflows could reach US$80-100bn in 2019 and US$120bn annually over 2020-30, with the CNY's share of global FX reserves rising from 2.5% to 5-10%.
