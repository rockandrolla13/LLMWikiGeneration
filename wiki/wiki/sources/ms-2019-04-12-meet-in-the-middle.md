# Corporate Credit Research: Meet in the Middle

**Authors:** Adam S. Richmond, Vishwas Patkar, Felician G. Stratmann, Frederick T. Fuchs, Jingli Li, Aleksandr Nozhnitskiy · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's credit team argues that short-duration investment grade has become rich in outright and relative terms after credit curves bull-steepened in early 2019, and recommends investors extend out the curve into 7-10Y bonds while reducing notionals. Front-end technicals may weaken as Fed pauses slow short-duration IG inflows, currency-hedged yields turn negative for non-US investors, and corporate Treasury demand fades. Late in the cycle, credit curves tend to bear-flatten from the last rate hike to the first cut as default and refinancing risks rise. Trade ideas include pairing low-dollar long-end bonds with short-dated CDS, 3s-5s CDS flatteners, and shorting the front end of CDX HY.

## Key Claims

1. Short-duration IG is rich after credit curves bull-steepened in 2019; investors should extend into 7-10Y bonds and reduce notionals for better risk/reward.
2. Credit curves tend to bear-flatten from the last rate hike to the first rate cut as the market brings forward default expectations and refinancing risk.
3. Front-end technicals are deteriorating, including near-zero currency-hedged IG yields for non-US (EUR/JPY) investors.
