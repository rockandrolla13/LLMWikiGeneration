# Sunday Start | What's Next in Global Macro: A Full-Court Policy Press

**Authors:** Chetan Ahya · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Ahya frames Covid-19 as an unparalleled synchronous demand and supply shock, forecasting a deep global recession in 1H20 with global growth contracting 0.6% for full-year 2020, the weakest peacetime pace since the 1930s. He argues the shock reinforces the pre-existing '3D Challenge' of demographics, debt and disinflation. The note stresses an unprecedented coordinated monetary and fiscal response, with G4 central banks launching aggressive QE (~US$6.5tn of purchases) and large fiscal deficits. Citing the 1930s and Japan, he warns policymakers against terminating stimulus prematurely.

## Key Claims

1. A deep global recession is expected in 1H20, with 2020 full-year global growth contracting 0.6%, the weakest peacetime pace since the 1930s.
2. Covid-19 brings the '3D Challenge' (demographics, debt, disinflation) back to the fore and is ultimately disinflationary.
3. An unprecedented coordinated monetary and fiscal response is underway, including ~US$6.5tn of G4 asset purchases and large primary deficit expansion.
4. Expansionary policy must remain in place until inflation expectations rise, citing the 1937-38 double dip as a cautionary tale.
