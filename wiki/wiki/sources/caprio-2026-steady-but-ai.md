# 2026: Steady, but AI & the Hawks are Circling (Software)

**Authors:** Steve Caprio, Jim Reid, Karthik Nagalingam, Cem Keltek, Emilie Calder · **Year:** 2026 · **Venue:** Deutsche Bank Research - Global Credit Strategy Default Study · **Type:** report

## Summary

Deutsche Bank Research's 2026 annual Default Study (2 June 2026) argues that while no major global default cycle is expected, aggregate data and tight prices understate persistent dispersion: US and European speculative-grade default rates should remain above long-term averages through Q2'27, with low recoveries and a prevalence of distressed exchanges signalling many overleveraged capital structures. In the US, the report forecasts a 'K-shaped' ('AI & ice') economy where AI tailwinds boost profits even as the typical business sees margin compression; the largest risk is software downgrades from AI disruption combined with potential Fed hikes squeezing floating-rate leveraged-loan and private-credit software issuers. In Europe, defaults fall but the ECB shifts from cuts to hikes, raising refinancing costs.

## Key Claims

1. US spec-grade default rates fell from a 5.1% peak to 4% but remain above the 20-year median of 2.9%; forecast 3.6% by YE'26 then 4% by Q2'27.
2. European spec-grade defaults rose to 4.6% in April 2026; forecast to settle at 3.6% by YE'26 and 3.9% by Q2'27.
3. Software downgrades are the largest US risk: 30% of US software leveraged loans trade at an implied CCC rating vs 10% from rating agencies; 80% of B-rated debt is financed via leveraged loans or private credit.
4. Distressed exchanges made up 68% of global spec-grade defaults in 2025, with very low recovery rates.
5. Real-rate regime matters: median B2/B3 default rates are far higher in high-real-rate regimes.
6. The ECB shifting from cuts to at least two hikes in 2026 raises European refinancing costs 70-170bps; 37% of B-rated European HY issuers are FCF-negative now.

## Questions Raised

- When will AI disruption of software business models translate into weaker fundamentals rather than just lower equity multiples?
- Will the Fed begin hiking at YE'26 / early 2027, combining tighter policy with AI-driven software stress?
- How large would private-credit loan markdowns be if forced software-loan sales materialize?
