# Introducing the Credit Bond Market Indicator (credit-BMI)

**Authors:** Meghan G. Robson, Adam S. Richmond, Vishwas Patkar, Frederick T. Fuchs, Felician G. Stratmann · **Year:** 2018 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley introduces the Credit Bond Market Indicator (credit-BMI), a framework for making tactical credit market calls based on four factors selected from about 60 tested variables: volatility, momentum, sentiment, and liquidity. Scores range from -10 (max short) to +10 (max long), with more extreme readings carrying higher conviction. The combined indicator has a 67% hit rate for IG and 62% for HY in predicting the one-month-ahead direction of excess returns, outperforming any single component thanks to low cross-factor correlations. At publication the model signaled being long credit over the next month, but with low conviction as the liquidity signal was cautious.

## Key Claims

1. The credit-BMI combines four standardized factors (volatility, momentum, sentiment, liquidity) selected from ~60 tested variables to predict 1M-ahead credit excess returns.
2. The combined indicator achieves a 67% hit rate for IG and 62% for HY, better than any single component due to low inter-factor correlations.
3. At publication the model signaled long credit with low conviction, as the liquidity/funding signal was cautious while volatility, momentum, and sentiment were positive.
