# Weekly Credit Wrap: High Yield — Pricing Tomorrow's Deleveraging Today

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Serena Tang, Jonathan Graber · **Year:** 2011 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

This Weekly Credit Wrap reviews European high yield fundamentals, finding that across 94 tracked issuers credit quality consolidated 4Q10 gains with improved interest coverage and cash flow, though deleveraging paused. Morgan Stanley expects more dispersion mid-cycle as margins peak and a strong euro and the rate cycle drive divergence in operating performance, and maintains an equal-weight on high yield while expecting it to underperform investment grade. The note recommends selling Main and buying Crossover protection in a 4:1 ratio and screens for bonds with favourable spread-adjusted valuations, solid free cash flow, and larger interest-coverage margins.

## Key Claims

1. Across 94 European high yield issuers, credit quality consolidated 4Q10 gains with over 70% reducing leverage YoY, though deleveraging progress paused.
2. Morgan Stanley maintains an equal-weight on high yield but expects it to underperform investment grade as valuations are tested.
3. The note recommends selling Main and buying Crossover (XOver) protection in a 4:1 ratio to position for high yield underperformance.
