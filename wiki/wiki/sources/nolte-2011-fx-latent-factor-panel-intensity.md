# Trading Dynamics in the Foreign Exchange Market: A Latent Factor Panel Intensity Approach

**Authors:** [[entities/ingmar-nolte|Ingmar Nolte]], [[entities/valeri-voev|Valeri Voev]]

**Venue:** *Journal of Financial Econometrics*, 9(4), 685-716 (2011)

**DOI:** [10.1093/jjfinec/nbq033](https://doi.org/10.1093/jjfinec/nbq033)

## Summary

Builds a multivariate [[concepts/panel-intensity-models|panel intensity model]] with a time-varying latent factor for trader actions (open/close positions) across investors and currency pairs in OANDA FXTrade data. Estimates by [[concepts/simulated-maximum-likelihood|simulated maximum likelihood]] with [[concepts/efficient-importance-sampling|efficient importance sampling]] and documents a [[concepts/disposition-effect|disposition effect]] at the portfolio level (not only single positions) that varies with investor sophistication.

## Key Claims

1. A latent-factor [[concepts/stochastic-conditional-intensity|stochastic conditional intensity]] model in a panel of investors and currency pairs jointly captures open and close decisions
2. Disposition effect operates at both single-position and total-portfolio level; large investors show portfolio-level but not position-level disposition
3. Higher current risk exposure leads to more cautious trading, consistent with high-frequency risk aversion
