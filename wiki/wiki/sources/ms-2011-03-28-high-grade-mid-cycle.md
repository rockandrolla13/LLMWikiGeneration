# Weekly Credit Wrap: High Grade, Mid-Cycle

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Serena Tang, Jonathan Graber · **Year:** 2011 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A European fixed income research weekly assessing investment-grade credit fundamentals as of 4Q10, finding broad-based improvement in credit quality and strong deleveraging, with median net debt/EBITDA at the lowest level of the decade. A composite of balance-sheet, M&A, and bank-lending metrics places the credit cycle near early-2004 levels, signaling a 'mid-cycle' environment. The report updates spread-per-leverage valuation measures, noting the market looks slightly cheap versus 10-year averages, and provides single-name screens of best and worst credits.

## Key Claims

1. Across 165 high grade issuers tracked, credit quality improved broadly through 4Q10 with the strongest deleveraging in a decade.
2. A composite of cycle metrics (balance sheet, M&A, bank lending) places the credit environment near early-2004 'mid-cycle' levels.
3. Reduced gearing has made the high grade market look slightly cheap on spread-per-leverage versus 10-year averages, though sector dispersion is large.
4. Risks from a stronger EUR, higher oil, and rising rates are expected to weigh more on high yield than high grade tactically.
