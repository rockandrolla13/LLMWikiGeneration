# Momentum for Diversification

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Wanting Low · **Year:** 2016 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A Cross-Asset Quant note evaluating cross-asset trend and momentum strategies, defined respectively as long/short positioning on the sign of 12-month returns and long/short on the best-versus-worst return quartile within an asset class. The authors find momentum and trend produce modest but respectable risk-adjusted returns, with the main appeal being diversification rather than outright return. Momentum can position for both higher and lower valuation dispersion, doing relatively better when stocks fall, which makes it a useful low-cost portfolio hedge. Given a macro backdrop of slow growth and easy policy, near-term return prospects for these factors are subdued, but they can capture large structural repricings that are hard to time.

## Key Claims

1. Cross-asset trend and momentum strategies produce modest, respectable risk/reward but are less compelling than carry.
2. Momentum's main value is diversification, performing relatively better when equities fall and acting as a low-cost portfolio hedge.
3. A macro backdrop of slow growth and easy monetary policy is not supportive of momentum and trend over the short term.
