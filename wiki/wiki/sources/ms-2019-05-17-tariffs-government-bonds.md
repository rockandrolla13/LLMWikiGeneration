# For Whom the Tariffs Toll

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach, Guneet Dhingra, David Harris, Kelcie Gerson, Tony Small, Shreya Chander, Robert J Brown, Koichi Sugisaki, Shoki Omori · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A Global Interest Rate Strategist weekly arguing that rising trade tensions raise downside risks to growth and global rates, with the most upside seen in US duration. The team stays bullish on US Treasuries and would buy dips driven by trade-deal hopes into the June 28-29 G20 summit, while discussing Italian BTP volatility, the euribor curve inversion, and bullish JGB long-end views. It also presents mid-year linker outlooks, money-market analysis of China's Treasury selling and swap spreads, and futures roll views for the June-September cycle.

## Key Claims

1. Rising trade tensions skew economic and rates risks to the downside; the firm favors buying dips in US Treasuries into the June 28-29 G20 summit.
2. The Treasury market is underpriced for Fed easing, as the Fed would likely open with a 50bp cut if higher tariffs persist 3-4 months.
3. Bund yields fell to year-lows near -0.13% but are still expected to rise into end-2019 and through 2020 despite pervasive uncertainty.
4. BTP volatility reflected concern over Italian issuance and coalition continuity, but unilateral fiscal change is constrained by the coalition and budget process.
