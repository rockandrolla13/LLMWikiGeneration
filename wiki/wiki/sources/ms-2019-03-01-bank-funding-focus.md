# European Economics Weekly — Bank Funding Focus

**Authors:** Matthew Pennill, Bruna Skarica, Jacob Nell, Jan Kozak, Joao Almeida, Daniele Antonucci · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A weekly European economics note from Morgan Stanley focused on ECB bank funding. The firm expects the ECB to remain dovish and possibly announce a TLTRO replacement, with discussion likely to gain grounding as staff projections for inflation and growth are revised down significantly. Euro area hard data, such as retail sales and industrial production, are expected to point to some stabilisation early in the year, though growth is not seen recovering until later in 2019. In the UK, attention is on the second meaningful Brexit vote scheduled by March 12 and the Irish backstop negotiations.

## Key Claims

1. The ECB should remain dovish and may announce a TLTRO replacement, with April perhaps more likely than March.
2. ECB staff projections for both inflation and growth are set to be revised down significantly, reflecting softer late-2018 data.
3. Euro area hard data may show some stabilisation early in the year, but the base case is that growth will not recover until later in 2019.
