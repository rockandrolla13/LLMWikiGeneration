# High- and Low-Frequency Correlations in European Government Bond Spreads and Their Macroeconomic Drivers

**Authors:** [[entities/simona-boffelli|Simona Boffelli]], [[entities/vasiliki-skintzi|Vasiliki D. Skintzi]], [[entities/giovanni-urga|Giovanni Urga]]

**Venue:** *Journal of Financial Econometrics*, 15(1), 62-105 (2017)

**DOI:** [10.1093/jjfinec/nbv023](https://doi.org/10.1093/jjfinec/nbv023)

## Summary

Combines [[concepts/dynamic-equicorrelation|DECO]] and [[concepts/midas-regression|MIDAS]] to model 15-minute high-frequency correlations and monthly macroeconomic-driven low-frequency correlations of European 10-year [[concepts/government-bond-spreads|government bond spreads]] vs Germany during the 2007-2012 [[concepts/sovereign-debt-crisis|sovereign debt crisis]]. Finds that increasing crisis-era spread correlations are mainly driven by changing market liquidity rather than macro fundamentals; the [[concepts/dcc-midas|DCC-MIDAS]] and [[concepts/garch-midas|GARCH-MIDAS]] machinery jointly handles the mixed-frequency dynamics.

## Key Claims

1. Bond-spread volatility is strongly linked to deteriorating macroeconomic fundamentals (employment, industrial production, economic sentiment)
2. Crisis-era correlation increases cannot be fully explained by macro fundamentals; market liquidity plays a dominant role
3. DECO-MIDAS provides a tractable framework for jointly modeling mixed-frequency, high-dimensional correlation dynamics
