# Add to Credit – and How Much Do Markets Lead the Economy?

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran L Naraparaju, Serena W Tang, Wanting Low, Naomi Z Poole · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A Cross-Asset Dispatches note from late March 2020 raising global credit to overweight via additions to US corporate and securitized credit, funded from cash and commodities. Using a five-point checklist (valuations, central bank action, volatility peaking, late-cycle dynamics, COVID-19 trajectory), the team argues enough progress has been made to add risk, particularly in credit. It examines how markets historically bottom in recessions, noting markets usually lead the economy, so a bottom may be forming even amid the worst growth quarter in US history. They prefer cash bonds over CDS indices given skew and basis, and see better risk/reward in global credit than global equities.

## Key Claims

1. Global credit raised to overweight (credit weight from +0 to +5) via adds to US corporate and securitized, funded by reducing cash and commodities.
2. Markets usually lead the economy in recessions, so a market bottom could typically be forming 'now' despite a likely record-bad 2Q20 for growth.
3. Unprecedented central bank action has eased USD funding stress, and volatility plus negative technical pressure may have peaked.
4. The team favours cash bonds over CDS indices in credit given skew and basis, and sees better risk/reward in global credit than global equities.
