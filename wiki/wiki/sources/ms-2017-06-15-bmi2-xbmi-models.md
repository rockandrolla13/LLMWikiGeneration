# Global Interest Rate Strategy: Introducing BMI(2) and xBMI, Improving BMI(10) and iBMI

**Authors:** Jesper Rooth, Federico Manicardi, Matthew Hornbach, Guneet Dhingra, Anton Heese, Koichi Sugisaki · **Year:** 2017 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley updates its Bond Market Indicators (BMI) framework, renaming the originals BMI(10), revising the bond-market momentum factor, and adding an FX/rates factor. It introduces BMI(2) for short-end mandates and a cross-market indicator (xBMI) that combines the BMI(10) signal differential with an FX factor to inform tactical cross-market calls. The iBMI inflation indicators are revised to use a logarithmic momentum weighting and a one-week observation shift to reduce dependence on the most recent data point; backtest performance is reported for each model.

## Key Claims

1. An FX/rates factor is added as a fifth BMI(10) factor and underpins the new xBMI cross-market indicator that blends signal differentials with FX.
2. BMI(2) replicates the BMI(10) structure recalibrated to the front end via volatility-adjusted carry, momentum, and the FX factor.
3. iBMI momentum is reweighted logarithmically and shifted by one week to avoid short-term return reversals.
