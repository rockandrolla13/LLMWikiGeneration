# Morgan Stanley FX Positioning Tracker

**Authors:** Gek Teng Khoo, Andrew M Watrous · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's FX Positioning Tracker (February 11, 2019) monitors G10 currency positioning using CFTC data, ETF flows, trader sentiment, and Japanese Toshin/TFX flows. As of early February the largest G10 short is in CAD and the largest long in USD, with USD long positioning increasing for a fifth consecutive week. The note flags that increasingly long USD positioning raises the risk of a sharp downside move if US data deteriorate, and recommends short AUD against BRL and JPY. CFTC estimates are noted as affected by data delays from the US government shutdown, so latest positioning is estimated via a regression model on spot FX moves.

## Key Claims

1. Within G10, the largest short position is in CAD and the largest long is in USD as of early February 2019.
2. USD long positioning increased for the fifth consecutive week, raising the risk of a sharp USD downside if US data deteriorate.
3. CAD, NZD and AUD sentiment and CFTC positioning were all reduced, consistent with a long-term bearish view on these countries' housing sectors.
4. Latest CFTC positioning is estimated using a linear regression model between spot FX and CFTC positioning over the past 6 months due to the US government shutdown.
