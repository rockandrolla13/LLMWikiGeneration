# Cross-Asset Dispatches: Could Dollar Weakness Be Self-Catalysing?

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran L Naraparaju, Serena W Tang, Wanting Low, Naomi Z Poole · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley maintains a bearish USD view, treating a weaker dollar as a key 2019 turning point driven by rich valuation and a shift in the US growth and policy narrative, reinforced by the January FOMC meeting. They argue dollar weakness could be self-reinforcing rather than limited by being 'widely expected', pointing to powerful forces favoring a downside overshoot. With FX hedging costs of US assets near record highs, foreign investors who previously wanted USD exposure may begin hedging or selling US assets once the dollar weakens, putting further downward pressure on USD. They recommend staying USD-bearish against DM and EM currencies and preferring rest-of-world equities over US equities.

## Key Claims

1. A major USD peak is in, driven by rich valuation and a shifting US growth/policy narrative.
2. Dollar weakness could be self-reinforcing as foreign investors reconsider poor FX-hedged US returns.
3. FX hedging costs of US assets back into local currency are near record highs.
4. Investors should stay USD-bearish across DM and EM and prefer RoW equities over US equities.

## Questions Raised

- How large could a self-reinforcing USD overshoot become if hedging-driven selling accelerates?
- Will high hedging costs actually trigger foreign rebalancing out of US assets?
