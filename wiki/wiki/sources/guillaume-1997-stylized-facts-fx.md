# From the Bird's Eye to the Microscope: Stylized Facts of Intra-daily FX Markets

**Authors:** Dominique M. Guillaume, Michel M. Dacorogna, Rakhal R. Dave, Ulrich A. Muller, Richard B. Olsen, Olivier V. Pictet

**Year:** 1997

**Venue:** Finance and Stochastics 1, 95-129

**Institutions:** London School of Economics, Oxford, Olsen & Associates (Zurich)

## Summary

A foundational survey of empirical regularities in intra-daily foreign exchange markets. The paper describes tick-by-tick data characteristics, proposes variable definitions, and groups empirical findings under three major topics: distribution of price changes, price formation process, and heterogeneous market structure.

## Key Contributions

### 1. Variable Definitions
- Price definitions (bid, ask, mid-price)
- Time scales (physical time, theta-time, intrinsic time)
- Volatility measures
- Spread characteristics
- Tick frequency and directional change frequency

### 2. Distribution of Price Changes
- Heavy tails (leptokurtosis)
- Scaling properties
- Non-normality even at high frequencies
- Aggregation does not quickly produce normality

### 3. Process of Price Formation
- Distinct characteristics at <10-15 minute frequencies
- Quote arrival patterns
- Spread dynamics
- Negative first-order autocorrelation of returns (bid-ask bounce)

### 4. Heterogeneous Market Structure
- 24-hour global market with three main centers (Tokyo, London, New York)
- Different agent types with varying time horizons
- Seasonal patterns reflecting geographic activity
- Technical analysis explained by heterogeneous interactions

## Data Description

- Olsen & Associates database (1987-1993)
- USD/DEM: 8.2 million quotes (~4,500/day)
- USD/JPY: 4.2 million quotes (~2,300/day)
- GBP/USD, USD/CHF: ~3.5 million each
- Reuters FXFX tick-by-tick quoted prices

## Key Findings

1. Intra-daily data reveals heterogeneous agent structure hidden in daily data
2. Market composed of traders with different time horizons, risk profiles, locations
3. Technical analysis success explained by heterogeneous agent interactions
4. Time scale definition is crucial (physical vs. event-based)
5. Market efficiency concept needs refinement at high frequencies

## Implications

- Models should account for agent heterogeneity
- Time scale choice affects observed properties
- Risk concepts need high-frequency refinement
- Learning processes differ across agent types
