# 4Q18 US Credit Fundamental Review

**Authors:** Adam Richmond, Vishwas Patkar, Fred Fuchs, Felician Stratmann, Aleks Nozhnitskiy, Jingli Li · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A brief update on investment grade, high yield, and leveraged loan fundamentals for 4Q18 US credit. Median IG gross leverage ticked lower to 2.28x as EBITDA grew 11.4%, though cash/debt fell to its lowest since 4Q08 and an earnings recession likely began in 1Q19. HY gross leverage declined to 4.07x while interest coverage improved to 4.67x, and leveraged loan new-issue 1L leverage rose to 4.18x in 2018.

## Key Claims

1. Median IG gross leverage declined to 2.28x in 4Q18 on 11.4% EBITDA growth, but remains above past pre-recession levels.
2. An earnings recession likely began in 1Q19, implying IG leverage should rise in 2019 from a higher base.
3. HY gross leverage dropped to 4.07x with interest coverage improving to 4.67x near 2015 peaks.
4. New-issue first-lien loan leverage rose to 4.18x in 2018 from 3.92x in 2017.
