# Our Bond Market Indicators: A Powerful Systematic Approach

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach, Anthony O'Brien, Anton Heese, Jesper Rooth, Kewei Yang, Geoffrey Kendrick, Kenro Kawano · **Year:** 2015 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley Global Rates Strategy product note (March 6, 2015) introducing a suite of Bond Market Indicators (MS BMI) used as systematic tools to guide and frame the debate on duration. The framework extracts value from individual signals related to sovereign bond excess returns, currently covering the US, UK, Germany and Japan. The model combines factors including volatility-adjusted carry, momentum, equity market performance, and the business cycle into a combined and overall score, where positive means long duration and negative means short. At publication, the indicators suggested a neutral stance toward duration across all four major sovereign markets.

## Key Claims

1. The MS Bond Market Indicators combine volatility-adjusted carry, momentum, equity market performance, and business cycle factors into a systematic duration signal.
2. The framework covers the US, UK, Germany and Japan sovereign bond markets.
3. Positive composite scores indicate long duration and negative scores indicate short duration.
4. At the time of publication, the indicators suggested a neutral duration stance across all four major sovereign markets.
