# Introduction to the Alaph Capital Liquid Macro Credit Fund

**Authors:** Shahraab Ahmad, Andreas Koukorinis · **Year:** 2014 · **Venue:** Alaph Capital (private fund presentation) · **Type:** presentation

## Summary

A July 2014 confidential investor pitch deck for the Alaph Capital Liquid Macro Credit Fund, an actively traded vehicle taking a top-down, medium-term view on global credit markets with a macro-product overlay. The fund runs a four-step process (macro themes, asset valuation, trade selection, portfolio construction) to rank relative-value opportunities by risk/reward, targeting a 1:2 risk-return ratio with one-week-to-three-month holds across liquid corporate and sovereign credit (cash bonds and CDS single names, indices, options). Risk management is centred on liquidity: a proprietary liquidity scoring mechanism ranks instruments by observable market inputs, targeting the ability to exit 80% of the portfolio within 15-20 trading days. A 'structural risk' framework treats investor redemption and funding as out-of-the-money options.

## Key Claims

1. The fund applies a top-down four-step process to systematically rank relative-value opportunities by risk/reward in global credit.
2. It targets a 1:2 risk-return ratio, one-week-to-three-month holds, and a ~70% North America / 30% Europe & EM split.
3. The eligible universe is liquid corporate and sovereign credit; no structured products.
4. Risk is managed through trade and portfolio construction rather than stop-losses alone, supported by a proprietary liquidity scoring mechanism.
5. Liquidity target: ability to exit at least 80% of the portfolio within 15-20 trading days.
6. Structural risk treats investor redemption and funding obligations as two out-of-the-money options.

## Questions Raised

- How robust is the pro-forma Hutchin Hill track record once carved out and fee-adjusted?
- Does the 80%-exit-in-15-20-days liquidity target hold during systemic stress?
- How is theme conviction quantified and converted into the risk-allocation matrix?
