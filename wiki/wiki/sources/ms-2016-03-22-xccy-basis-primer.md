# Global Interest Rate Strategy: XCCY Basis Primer

**Authors:** Alexander Wojt, Koichi Sugisaki · **Year:** 2016 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A primer on cross-currency (XCCY) basis swaps covering their mechanics, drivers, and risks. It defines an XCCY basis swap as a floating/floating swap where two parties simultaneously borrow and lend in two different currencies, with the basis quoted on the non-USD leg and reflecting the relative price of liquidity across currencies. The report explains quotation conventions, cash flows, and basis calculation, then examines drivers such as foreign-currency issuance and asset purchases, FX, rates, liquidity, and regulation, with additional material on decomposing the basis and comparing FX forwards to XCCY swaps.

## Key Claims

1. An XCCY basis swap is a floating/floating swap in which parties simultaneously borrow and lend in two different currencies, with the basis quoted on the non-USD leg.
2. The XCCY basis reflects the relative price of liquidity in one currency versus another.
3. Key drivers and risks of the basis include foreign-currency issuance and asset purchases, FX, rates, liquidity, and regulation.
