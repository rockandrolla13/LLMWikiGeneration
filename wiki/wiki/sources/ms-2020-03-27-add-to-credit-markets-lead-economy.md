# Cross-Asset Dispatches: Add to Credit — and How Much Do Markets Lead the Economy?

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran L Naraparaju, Serena W Tang, Wanting Low, Naomi Z Poole · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley raises global credit to overweight via additions to US corporate and securitized exposure, arguing checklist concerns (valuations, central bank action, volatility peaking, late-cycle dynamics) have improved. They expect the forecast severe US recession to shift their cycle model from 'downturn' into early-cycle 'repair', historically favorable for risk assets and especially credit. Citing that markets typically lead the economy, they argue a market bottom would historically be forming 'now' even with 2Q20 potentially the worst growth quarter in US history. They raise overall credit weight from +0 to +5, funding via cuts to cash and commodities, and prefer cash bonds over CDS indices given skew and basis.

## Key Claims

1. Morgan Stanley upgrades global credit to overweight, raising credit weight from +0 to +5 via US corporate and securitized adds.
2. Markets typically lead the economy, so a bottom would historically be forming despite a severe forecast recession.
3. Their cycle model shifting from 'downturn' to early-cycle 'repair' is historically better for risk assets, especially credit.
4. They favour cash bonds over CDS indices in credit given skew and basis considerations.

## Questions Raised

- How reliable are historical market lead times over the economy in an unprecedented pandemic recession?
- Will the US COVID-19 trajectory track worse than the base case and invalidate the upgrade?
