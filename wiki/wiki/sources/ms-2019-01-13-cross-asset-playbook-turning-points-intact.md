# Cross-Asset Playbook – January 2019: Turning Points Intact

**Authors:** Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Serena Tang, Wanting Low, Naomi Poole · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's January 2019 cross-asset note maintains its defensive positioning into the new year, citing decelerating growth, higher core inflation, tighter policy, political uncertainty and late-cycle signals. The team expects meaningful deceleration in global growth and EPS data in 1Q and reaffirms its 'turning point' thesis in US vs. rest-of-world performance. They raise EM credit by +1% (funded from cash) on the best risk-adjusted returns in their framework, while waiting for more fundamental support before a broader risk upgrade. The trade portfolio carries modestly positive equity beta and benefits from a weaker dollar and lower US rates.

## Key Claims

1. Decelerating growth, higher core inflation, tighter policy and late-cycle signals persist into 2019.
2. Global growth and EPS data should decelerate meaningfully in 1Q, partly due to hard comparisons.
3. EM credit is raised +1% from cash given the best risk-adjusted returns in the framework.
4. Short USD, long EM vs. US, and value vs. growth outperformed in December, suggesting turning points in those relationships.
