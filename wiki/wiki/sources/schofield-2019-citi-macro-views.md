# Citi Macro Views: Global Strategy and Macro Theme Book, Q1 2019

**Authors:** Mark Schofield, Benjamin Nabarro · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Citi Research (Citigroup Global Markets) · **Type:** report

## Summary

A Q1 2019 sell-side global macro strategy compendium published by Citi Research, synthesizing Citi's cross-asset and economics research into eight core macro themes and asset-allocation views as of mid-February 2019. The eight themes: (1) from QE to QT, with global central-bank net asset purchases turning negative in 2019; (2) falling economic resilience with fragile markets; (3) debt approaching the barrier, with corporate leverage above 2002/2009 levels and post-crisis spread compression concentrated in lower-quality assets; (4) inflation with cyclical tailwinds but structural uncertainty; (5) EM financial resilience but long-term risk; (6) trade and globalisation; (7) the future of Europe; and (8) China's Belt and Road. Citi neutralizes its US equity overweight and overweights EM versus DM.

## Key Claims

1. For the first time since 2008, global central-bank net asset purchases are likely negative in 2019.
2. Central-bank policy remains highly divergent, and divergence plus rising cross-asset correlations raises the risk of non-linear asset re-pricing.
3. Corporate leverage is above 2002 and 2009 levels; post-crisis spread compression concentrated borrowing-cost reductions in lower-quality assets, eroding resilience.
4. Sustained growth has relied on falling real rates and a shift from private to public debt, both possibly running out of room.
5. Inflation faces cyclical upside but structural fetters cloud the long-term outlook.
6. Europe is 'running out of road': soft data, near-zero monetary space, and populist politics make fiscal reform difficult.

## Questions Raised

- Will the real-economy impact of QT and divergent policy be limited, or trigger a reversal?
- Can advanced economies cope with higher rates given elevated debt-service burdens?
- Can European politics deliver fiscal reform without serious disruption first?
