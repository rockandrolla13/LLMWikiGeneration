# Global Interest Rate Strategist: Toward More Tightening

**Authors:** Matthew Hornbach, Guneet Dhingra, Sean Wan, Sam Elprince, Elaine Lin, Shreya Chander, Koichi Sugisaki, Shoki Omori, Jesper Rooth · **Year:** 2018 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Ahead of expected hawkish Fed and BoE meetings, Morgan Stanley positions for tightening via UST 2s30s flatteners and short 10y TIPS breakevens, and via UKT 5s30s and UKTi 10s30s flatteners. The firm expects a larger increase in the 2018/2019 mean Fed dots than in 2020/longer-run dots, and maintains periphery euro-area longs (BTP and Spain vs OAT and Bund on a beta-weighted basis). It argues fundamental, technical, and valuation factors have aligned to support short back-end breakeven positions.

## Key Claims

1. Both the Fed and the BoE are expected to deliver hawkish messages, favoring curve flatteners and short inflation breakeven positions.
2. The 2018/2019 mean Fed dots are expected to rise more than the 2020/longer-run mean dots.
3. Fundamental, technical, and valuation factors have aligned to support short 10-year breakeven positions, especially in the back end.
