# Assessing Risk Premia in EMFX (Part 1): A Two-Factor Model Approach

**Authors:** Andres Jaime · **Year:** 2018 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

An EM Quant Strategy note presenting a two-factor model (USD and a carry-trade factor) to decompose EMFX returns into systemic and idiosyncratic variance, the latter interpreted as local/idiosyncratic risk premia. The two factors explain close to 50% of systemic EMFX variation while avoiding the overfitting and misspecification of conventional fair-value models. The framework helps gauge extreme pricing as a potential trading signal and assess event impacts on local assets. On a volatility-adjusted basis, CNY, ZAR and THB show the lowest risk premia while CZK, HUF and TRY show the highest, with only THB flagged as a potential trade.

## Key Claims

1. A two-factor model using only USD and a carry-trade factor explains close to 50% of the systemic variation in EMFX.
2. Variation not explained by global factors is attributed to local idiosyncratic risk premia, which can be accumulated and tracked over time.
3. On a volatility-adjusted scale, CNY/ZAR/THB have the lowest risk premia and CZK/HUF/TRY the highest, with only THB a potential trade.
4. USD's share of EMFX variance has more than doubled post-crisis, acting as a barometer of risk-taking capacity via the financial channel and CIP deviations.
