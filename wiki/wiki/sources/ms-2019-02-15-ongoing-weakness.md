# European Economics Weekly — Ongoing Weakness

**Authors:** Jacob Nell, Jan Kozak, Joao Almeida, Matthew Pennill, Bruna Skarica, Daniele Antonucci · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A weekly European economics note from Morgan Stanley anticipating continued data weakness in the euro area and a focus on the ECB's view in its minutes. The firm expects PMIs to rise slightly on services support but projects the German Ifo and ZEW surveys to decline, and now sees the first ECB rate hike only in 2020 given below-trend growth lasting longer. In the UK, robust data is expected but seen as reflecting past strength rather than a rebound, with Brexit talks ongoing ahead of a Parliamentary debate scheduled for February 27.

## Key Claims

1. The firm expects continued declines in euro area data, with the German Ifo and ZEW surveys projected to fall.
2. Below-trend euro area growth lasting longer makes the ECB more dovish; the first rate hike is now expected only in 2020.
3. UK data is expected to be robust but reflects past strength rather than a rebound from recent weakness.
