# ECB Preview: TLTRO Funding on its Way

**Authors:** Daniele Antonucci, Joao Almeida, Robert J Brown · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley previews the ECB meeting, expecting the central bank to focus on replacing its long-term refinancing operations with new bank funding (TLTRO replacement), announced either in March or April. Downgrades to growth and inflation staff projections are seen as the backdrop that justifies action, with revisions showing subtrend growth and a weaker core-inflation pick-up. The Governing Council is expected to assess whether the banking system can still transmit monetary impulses adequately and to act to address financial-fragmentation risks. Mitigating these risks is framed as key to preserving pass-through of policy easing to the real economy via low lending rates.

## Key Claims

1. The ECB is likely to announce TLTRO replacement funding at either the March or April meeting.
2. Weaker staff projections showing subtrend growth and softer core inflation provide the context to act.
3. Addressing financial-fragmentation risk is key to preserving monetary-policy pass-through to lending rates.
4. The Governing Council will scrutinise the banking system's monetary-transmission capacity.
