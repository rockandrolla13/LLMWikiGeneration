# European Credit Strategy: Corporate Hybrids Playbook — Staying in Short Calls, Revisiting Call Risks

**Authors:** Srikanth Sankaran, Max Blass, Aron Becker · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley updates its European corporate hybrids playbook after the asset class suffered its worst total returns in a decade in 2018 and rebounded in January 2019, matching HY year-to-date. They favor short-call paper and avoid risk layering, noting that gappy price action has increased focus on call risks but expecting both ratings-focused and tactical issuers to execute timely calls. The note provides supply forecasts, relative-value screens versus seniors and BBs, and a hybrid monitor and features cheat sheet.

## Key Claims

1. Corporate hybrids posted their worst total returns in a decade in 2018, then rebounded in January 2019 to match HY year-to-date.
2. Short-call paper is preferred and risk layering avoided; both ratings-focused and tactical issuers are expected to execute timely calls.
3. The remaining rated 2019 call pipeline is a modest EUR 4.1bn while hybrids reaching first call dates in 2020 are worth about EUR 13bn.
4. 2019 hybrid supply is forecast at EUR 20-25bn across all currencies versus EUR 20bn issued in 2018; hybrids look slightly attractive versus seniors but less compelling versus BBs.
