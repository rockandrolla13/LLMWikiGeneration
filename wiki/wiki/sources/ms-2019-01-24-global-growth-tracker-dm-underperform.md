# Global Growth Tracker – DMs Underperform More than Expected

**Authors:** Chetan Ahya, Derrick Y Kam, Nora Wassermann · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's Global Growth Tracker (January 24, 2019) reports that developed-market growth is surprising to the downside earlier and more sharply than expected, dragging global GDP growth to a below-trend 3.4%Y in both 4Q18 and 1Q19. The team projects US 4Q/4Q 2019 growth of 1.7%, well below the 2.2% consensus, citing a maturing cycle, fading fiscal stimulus, past monetary tightening and idiosyncratic factors such as the US government shutdown. In contrast, growth in China (improving from 2Q19) and other EMs is holding up, reaffirming the call that growth differentials will swing in EMs' favour. Three policy anchors are flagged: China easing, Fed flexibility, and a US-China trade deal.

## Key Claims

1. DM growth is surprising to the downside earlier and more sharply than expected, dragging global GDP growth to a below-trend 3.4%Y for both 4Q18 and 1Q19.
2. US 4Q/4Q 2019 growth is projected at 1.7%, well below the 2.2% consensus, with the government shutdown directly cutting quarterly GDP by ~0.05pp per week.
3. Growth in EMs excluding China is holding up well, supported by softer oil prices, a weaker USD and lower US yields easing financial conditions.
4. The growth outlook hinges on three policy anchors: the pace and effectiveness of China's easing, Fed flexibility on rates and the balance sheet, and a US-China trade deal.
