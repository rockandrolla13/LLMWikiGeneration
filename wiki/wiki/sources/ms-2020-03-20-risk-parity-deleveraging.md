# Cross-Asset Brief: Risk Parity — Worst Behind Us

**Authors:** Phanikiran L Naraparaju, Wanting Low, Andrew Sheets, Serena W Tang, Naomi Z Poole · **Year:** 2020 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley estimates that the March 2020 spike in cross-asset volatility and correlation drove historically extreme deleveraging in risk parity portfolios, with portfolio leverage falling from 2.0x in December to 1.1x. Equity exposure in their model fell near all-time lows (21MM per 100MM notional, from ~80MM a month earlier) and rates exposure dropped to 87MM (from ~145MM). They argue the worst of the risk parity outflows is likely behind, especially for stocks, with smaller and more spread-out incremental outflows expected. They flag a less-negative stock-bond correlation as a watch item that could trigger modest further deleveraging.

## Key Claims

1. Risk parity portfolio leverage collapsed from 2.0x in December to 1.1x as equity and rates volatility spiked.
2. Risk parity equity exposure dropped to near all-time lows (~21MM from ~80MM per 100MM notional).
3. The worst of risk parity outflows is likely behind, with smaller incremental outflows ahead especially for stocks.
4. Risk parity deleveraging helps explain the unusual positive stock-bond correlation observed recently.

## Questions Raised

- Will a persistently less-negative stock-bond correlation trigger further deleveraging?
- How vulnerable are rates to a switch out of bonds into stocks as equity vol eases relative to rates?
