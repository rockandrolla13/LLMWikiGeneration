# Realized Probability Index is a Better Market Timing Indicator

**Authors:** [[entities/haibin-xie|Haibin Xie]], [[entities/boyao-wu|Boyao Wu]], [[entities/yuying-sun|Yuying Sun]], [[entities/shouyang-wang|Shouyang Wang]]

**Venue:** *Studies in Nonlinear Dynamics and Econometrics*, 30(1), 23-36 (2026)

**DOI:** [10.1515/snde-2024-0060](https://doi.org/10.1515/snde-2024-0060)

## Summary

Compares three [[concepts/market-timing|market-timing]] indicators (return, 0-1 binary directional, realized probability) and proves the [[concepts/realized-probability-index|realized probability index]], constructed from [[concepts/high-frequency-data|high-frequency intraday data]] as the share of cumulative positive returns in cumulative absolute return, is more informative than the binary indicator and more predictable than the return itself. Links to the [[concepts/sign-dependence-volatility|Christoffersen-Diebold]] sign-dependence-via-volatility line of work on [[concepts/directional-forecasting|directional forecasting]].

## Key Claims

1. The realized probability index dominates the 0-1 binary indicator in information content and economic efficiency
2. The realized probability index is more predictable than the return itself, making it attractive for practical timing strategies
3. Empirical results on multiple equity indices confirm the theoretical efficiency ordering
