# European Banks - Equity & Credit: Untested Cycle - watch for corporate risk

**Authors:** Magdalena L Stoklosa, Izabel Dobreva, Antonio Reale, Srikanth Sankaran, Max S Blass, Bruce Hamilton, Alvaro Serrano, Giulia Aurora Miotto, Alice Timperley · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley warns that a deteriorating European credit cycle will have a tangible impact on banks' earnings into 2019/20, with corporate (not consumer) exposure the key risk, concentrated in export- and manufacturing-led economies (Germany, Italy, Belgium, Netherlands). Default rates are expected to rise from current lows, with European high yield defaults forecast at 2.5-3% by year-end in a post-QE, more selective funding environment, and corporate ratings drift has worsened since mid-2018. The team assumes sector provisions rise ~40% by 2021e, sits ~6% below consensus, stays defensive, and prefers resilient names: Santander, Credit Agricole, Lloyds, HSBC, KBC.

## Key Claims

1. A deteriorating corporate credit cycle will materially affect European banks' earnings into 2019/20, with risk concentrated in export- and manufacturing-led economies (Germany, Italy, Belgium, Netherlands).
2. European high yield default rates are forecast to rise to 2.5-3% by year-end as post-QE funding markets become more selective and ratings drift worsens.
3. Total sector provisions are assumed to rise ~40% by 2021e (cost of risk from ~30bps in 2018 to ~40bps in 2021e), keeping the team ~6% below consensus.
4. Banks have been structurally disintermediated by deeper corporate bond markets and private credit, but stagnant operating profits leave them vulnerable to credit deterioration.
