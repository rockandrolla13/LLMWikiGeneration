# China – Doing whatever it takes

**Authors:** Chetan Ahya, Robin Xing, Derrick Y Kam, Nora Wassermann · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's global economics team pushes back against investor skepticism about China's growth outlook, arguing that intensifying policy easing since December 2018 will drive a growth improvement from 2Q19. Past easing failed because it was subdued through November 2018 while policymakers prioritised financial stability, and because measures aimed at the private sector were blunted by regulatory tightening and trade tensions. With the labour market now at risk, policymakers have shifted to pushing the public sector to lift spending. This supports the team's key 2019 macro call that emerging markets retake the lead in driving global growth.

## Key Claims

1. China's policy easing has intensified and changed in nature since December 2018, with the public sector now pushed to do the heavy lifting on spending.
2. Growth is expected to improve from 2Q19 with broad credit growth accelerating to 12.5%Y by year-end (versus 10.6% now) and the augmented fiscal deficit widening by ~150bps in 2019.
3. Past easing efforts failed because they were subdued until Nov-18 amid a financial-stability focus and were aimed at a private sector dampened by regulation and trade tensions.
4. Skepticism about China's growth typically peaks right around the trough of the growth cycle, as seen in 2015-16 and 2017.
