# Cross-Asset Dispatches: Are You Sure That's 'Low Beta'?

**Authors:** Wanting Low, Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran L Naraparaju, Naomi Z Poole, Serena W Tang · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley challenges conventional wisdom on which assets are genuinely 'defensive', noting that asset class betas shift materially over time. They build a scorecard ranking assets on three components: their beta to global equities, the stability of that beta, and the ability of that beta to hold when the market sells off. They highlight that the US dollar, US versus rest-of-world equities, and growth versus value have all acted like high-beta assets on a trailing three-year basis despite being commonly viewed as low beta. They identify utilities, staples and RoW equities within equities, and G3 rates, JPY and DM IG credit within fixed income, as reliably low-beta assets.

## Key Claims

1. Asset class betas shift materially over time, so static assumptions about 'defensive' assets can mislead.
2. USD, US-vs-RoW equities and growth-vs-value have recently behaved as high-beta assets despite low-beta reputations.
3. A robust low-beta scorecard combines beta level, beta stability, and beta persistence in sell-offs.
4. Utilities, staples, RoW equities, G3 rates, JPY and DM IG credit screen as reliably low beta.

## Questions Raised

- How frequently should defensive asset classifications be re-estimated as betas drift?
- Does a high downside beta in supposedly defensive assets undermine standard hedging assumptions?
