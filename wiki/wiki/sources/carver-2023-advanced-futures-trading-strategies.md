# Advanced Futures Trading Strategies

**Authors:** Robert Carver · **Year:** 2023 · **Venue:** Harriman House · **Type:** book

## Summary

Robert Carver presents 30 fully systematic futures trading strategies, all rule-based, using only price data, and tested across 100+ futures instruments spanning equities, bonds, currencies, and commodities with up to 50+ years of history. The book is built on a consistent volatility-based risk-scaling methodology so strategies can be mixed within one diversified portfolio. It progresses from basic directional strategies (buy-and-hold, trend following, carry) through advanced trend/carry variants, advanced directional strategies (breakout, value, skew, dynamic optimisation), fast mean-reversion, and relative-value strategies (spreads, calendar trades). Carver's central thesis is that diversification across instruments, strategies, and time frames is the most powerful tool available to a systematic trader.

## Key Claims

1. Diversification across instruments, strategies, and time frames is the most powerful lever for improving risk-adjusted returns.
2. Strategies should be tested across a wide universe (100+ instruments, up to 50+ years) to ensure they are not curve-fit.
3. Position sizing should be scaled to a target risk (volatility) so strategies can be combined consistently.
4. Trend following and carry are the two foundational return sources in futures markets and can be combined for robustness.
5. Objective, rule-based strategies using only closing price data are preferable to discretionary or fundamental inputs.
6. Tactical execution concerns - contract selection, rolling, execution quality, cash management - materially affect realised returns.

## Questions Raised

- Should each asset class be traded with bespoke rules, or do common strategies generalise across all futures markets?
- When capital is insufficient for the full portfolio, how can dynamic optimisation approximate the diversified target?
