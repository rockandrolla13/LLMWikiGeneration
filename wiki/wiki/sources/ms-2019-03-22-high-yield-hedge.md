# A High Yield Hedge

**Authors:** Adam S Richmond, Vishwas Patkar, Frederick T Fuchs, Felician G Stratmann, Aleksandr Nozhnitskiy, Jingli Li · **Year:** 2019 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A North America Corporate Credit and Derivatives Research note recommending buying protection on short-dated high-yield CDX (CDX HY S25) as a hedge against rising defaults and default expectations over the next two years. The team argues high yield is not insulated from broader credit-cycle risks despite its post-2016 deleveraging and shrinking par outstanding. With front-end CDX HY priced for very few defaults, shorting HY25 to expiry profits if just ~4 defaults occur over ~2 years at 30% recovery. The trade also works as a positively convex, low-carry way to position for a large credit sell-off, since HY credit curves tend to flatten in stress.

## Key Claims

1. Buying protection on short-dated HY CDX is a good way to position for rising defaults and default expectations over the next two years, a risk not in the price.
2. Shorting CDX HY25 to expiry profits if the index sees just ~4 defaults over ~2 years assuming 30% recovery, an unusually low rate even in benign conditions.
3. High yield remains highly sensitive to growth expectations and credit conditions despite delevering more than IG and loans since 2016.
4. Short-dated HY CDX is a positively convex, lower-holding-cost way to position for a sell-off, as HY credit curves flatten meaningfully in large sell-offs.
