# Global In the Flow: October Recap

**Authors:** Wanting Low, Andrew Sheets, Phanikiran Naraparaju, Serena Tang, Naomi Poole · **Year:** 2018 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's cross-asset team recaps a turbulent October 2018, in which global equities saw a roughly 2-sigma move, leaving the global index down 3.5% YTD and 10.1% off January highs; the S&P 500 fell 6.8% and EM equities fell 8.7%. Rates, FX, and credit moves were comparatively orderly, with UST 10Y total returns at -0.4% and US HY excess returns at -1.6%. Within equities, value outperformed growth and utilities led sectors. Sentiment moved to fear territory, with the GRDI at -1.8 and the VIX rising to 21.2%, while DM HY and EM credit supply fell.

## Key Claims

1. Global equities experienced an approximately 2-sigma move in October 2018, with the S&P 500 down 6.8% and EM equities down 8.7%.
2. Value outperformed growth within global equities during the October selloff, signaling a rotation.
3. Sentiment indicators turned to fear, with the MS Global Risk Demand Index at -1.8 and the VIX rising to 21.2%.
