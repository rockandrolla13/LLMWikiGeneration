# Europe Economics: Recession Returns

**Authors:** Elga Bartsch, Daniele Antonucci, Olivier Bizimana, Tomasz Pietrzak · **Year:** 2011 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

A Europe Economics note cutting Morgan Stanley's euro-area GDP forecasts as fresh downside risks from additional austerity and rising funding costs take hold, with 2012 real GDP now expected to fall 0.2% below the 2011 average versus a prior 0.5% growth forecast. Economic activity is projected to shrink roughly 0.75% through mid-2012 before a modest recovery, with first 2013 growth pegged at 0.9%. Offsetting factors include further ECB easing, a weaker EUR, robust global growth and strong corporate balance sheets. The authors expect the ECB to cut the refi rate to a historic 50bp low in early 2012 and view large-scale, unsterilized government bond purchases (QE) as a high-hurdle last resort requiring a political solution first.

## Key Claims

1. Morgan Stanley cuts its 2012 euro-area real GDP forecast to -0.2% (from +0.5%), expecting activity to contract about 0.75% through mid-2012.
2. The ECB is expected to cut the refi rate to a historic low of 50bp in early 2012, with broad-based QE only a high-hurdle last resort needing a political solution.
3. Offsetting headwinds include ECB easing, a weaker EUR, robust global growth and strong corporate balance sheets, though risks are asymmetrically tilted to the downside.
