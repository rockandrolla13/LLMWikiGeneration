# US Interest Rate Strategy: Funding Market Signals (Correction)

**Authors:** Sam Elprince, Matthew Hornbach, Sean Wan · **Year:** 2018 · **Venue:** Morgan Stanley Research · **Type:** report

## Summary

Morgan Stanley's US rates strategists analyze funding-market dynamics, noting that demand for risk-free short-term agency debt and T-bills picked up, signaling tighter FRA-OIS as Federal Home Loan Banks channel funding to banks. They decompose the Libor-OIS spread into a credit-spread component (Libor-Repo or Libor-T-bill) and a risk-free funding-demand component (Repo-OIS or T-bill-OIS), finding T-bill issuance could explain over 40% of the Libor-OIS move since November 2017. With commercial paper outstanding falling and FHLB funding spreads tightening 11bp, they recommend 2y swap spread tighteners as an attractive entry point. The note is a correction to a prior publication's 3m Repo rate series.

## Key Claims

1. The Libor-OIS spread can be decomposed into a credit-spread component (Libor-Repo/Libor-T-bill) and a risk-free funding-demand component (Repo-OIS/T-bill-OIS).
2. T-bill issuance could explain over 40% of the move in Libor-OIS since November 2017, with CP issuance and wider credit spreads accounting for the rest.
3. 2y swap spread tighteners look attractive as 3m T-bill-OIS tightened 11bp from its March peak while Libor-OIS remained wide.

## Questions Raised

- What factors (e.g., reversal in CD-OIS spreads, rising CP issuance, or a credit risk event) could cause FRA-OIS to widen contrary to expectations?
