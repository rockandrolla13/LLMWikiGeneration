# Martin D. Gould

**Martin D. Gould** is a quantitative researcher specializing in market microstructure, limit order books, and the statistical properties of financial markets. His work combines rigorous empirical analysis with mathematical modeling.

## Affiliations

- CFM-Imperial Institute of Quantitative Finance, Imperial College London
- Mathematical Institute, University of Oxford
- Oxford-Man Institute of Quantitative Finance

## Research Focus

### Limit Order Book Dynamics
- Empirical properties of LOBs
- Order flow statistics
- Price formation mechanisms

### Long Memory in Markets
- Autocorrelation properties of order flow
- Testing for true vs spurious long memory
- FX market microstructure

### Multi-Level Order Flow
- Beyond best bid/ask analysis
- Deep LOB contribution to price formation
- MLOFI methodology

## Key Contributions

### Long Memory in FX
[[sources/gould-2016-long-memory-fx|Gould et al. (2016)]]:
- High-quality Hotspot FX data analysis
- Confirmed H ≈ 0.7 for order flow
- Single-day estimation (no cross-day aggregation)
- Rejected spurious long-memory hypothesis

### Multi-Level Order Flow Imbalance
[[sources/xu-2020-mlofi|Xu et al. (2020)]]:
- Extended OFI to multiple price levels
- Ridge regression for correlated MLOFI components
- Demonstrated deep LOB influence on prices
- LOBSTER data for Nasdaq stocks

### LOB Survey
Author of influential survey paper on limit order books (Gould et al., 2013), which comprehensively reviewed:
- Order book mechanics
- Stylized facts
- Mathematical models
- Empirical findings

## Research Approach

Gould's work is characterized by:

1. **High-Quality Data:** Uses institutional-grade datasets
2. **Statistical Rigor:** Multiple estimation methods
3. **Hypothesis Testing:** Formal statistical tests
4. **Practical Relevance:** Connections to trading applications
