# Alexander Barzykin

## Overview

Alexander Barzykin is a quantitative practitioner at **HSBC**, based in London. His work bridges academic research and practical implementation in foreign exchange (FX) market making, bringing real-world data and insights to the development of algorithmic trading models.

## Industry-Academic Collaboration

Barzykin represents a unique bridge between industry practice and academic theory:
- Provides access to proprietary trading data (anonymized HSBC FX data)
- Validates theoretical models against real market conditions
- Brings practical constraints and objectives to academic models

## Research Contributions

### FX Market Making
Barzykin has co-authored several papers establishing the modern framework for FX market making:
- Introduction of [[concepts/internalization-externalization]] dilemma to academic models
- Empirical validation of client tiering using real trading data
- Multi-currency portfolio approaches

### Key Innovations
- **Pure internalization threshold**: Proved existence of inventory levels below which externalization is suboptimal
- **Client tiering validation**: Used k-means clustering on real data to identify distinct client behaviors
- **Practical parameterization**: Provided realistic parameters for model calibration
