# Rose Yu

**Rose Yu** is a faculty member at the University of California, San Diego, where she leads the **Spatiotemporal Learning (STL) Lab**. Her research focuses on machine learning for spatiotemporal data, with applications in transportation, climate science, and healthcare.

## Research Contributions

### Uncertainty Quantification
- **CopulaCPTS** ([[sources/sun-2022-copula-cpts]]) - Copula-based conformal prediction for multi-step time series forecasting, published at ICLR 2024
- Deep uncertainty quantification for weather forecasting
- Probabilistic spatiotemporal forecasting

### Key Research Areas
1. Spatiotemporal machine learning
2. Physics-informed deep learning
3. Uncertainty quantification for neural networks
4. Time series forecasting
5. Autonomous systems

## STL Lab

The Spatiotemporal Learning Lab develops methods for:
- Multi-step forecasting with uncertainty
- Trajectory prediction (Argoverse benchmark)
- COVID-19 forecasting
- Weather and climate prediction

Notable papers:
- Quantifying uncertainty in deep spatiotemporal forecasting (KDD 2021)
- TACTIS: Transformer-attentional copulas for time series (Drouin et al., 2022)

## Research Philosophy

From the CopulaCPTS work:
- Emphasizes the importance of **calibrated** uncertainty (coverage matching specified confidence level)
- Focus on **efficiency** (small confidence regions) alongside validity
- Distribution-free methods that work with any underlying model

## Collaborators

- [[entities/sophia-sun|Sophia Sun]] - PhD student, CopulaCPTS

## Impact

Her lab's work addresses critical applications:
- Safe autonomous vehicles (trajectory prediction with uncertainty)
- Pandemic modeling (COVID-19 forecasting)
- Energy systems (renewable power forecasting)
