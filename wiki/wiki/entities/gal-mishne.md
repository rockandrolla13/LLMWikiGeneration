# Gal Mishne

**Affiliation:** University of California San Diego
**Role:** Professor
**Department:** Electrical and Computer Engineering

## Research Focus

Gal Mishne is a professor at UC San Diego whose research spans [[concepts/graph-signal-processing|graph signal processing]], machine learning, and manifold learning. Her work focuses on developing principled methods for learning from structured and geometric data.

## Research Themes

### Graph Learning
- [[concepts/graph-laplacian|Graph Laplacian]] inference from observations
- Product graph structures and decompositions
- Non-Gaussian noise models for graph learning

### Manifold Learning
- Geometric approaches to data analysis
- Manifold-based adversarial defense
- Self-supervised representation learning

### Signal Processing
- Graph signal denoising
- Multi-way signal processing
- Spectral methods for structured data

## Research Contributions

### GLEN Framework
Developed methods for graph inference that extend beyond Gaussian noise assumptions to handle:
- Poisson-distributed count data
- Binomial data
- Other exponential family distributions

### Product Graph Learning
Theoretical and algorithmic contributions to learning:
- Cartesian product graphs
- Kronecker product graphs
- Strong product graphs

### Adversarial Robustness
Novel approaches using manifold geometry and self-supervised learning to defend neural networks against adversarial attacks.
