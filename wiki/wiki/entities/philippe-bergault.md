# Philippe Bergault

## Overview

Philippe Bergault is a researcher affiliated with **Université Paris Dauphine-PSL** (CEREMADE) and previously **École Polytechnique** (CMAP). His research focuses on [[concepts/market-making]] models, particularly the mathematical foundations of optimal quoting strategies in OTC markets.

## Research Focus

Bergault's work spans several key areas:
- **Multi-asset market making**: Closed-form approximations and dimensionality reduction
- **FX market making**: Client tiering, internalization vs. externalization
- **RFQ markets**: Micro-pricing and fair transfer prices
- **Informational risk**: Adverse selection and price reading

## Key Contributions

### Computational Methods
- Development of closed-form proxies for multi-asset value functions
- Application of Riccati equations for efficient strategy computation
- Monte-Carlo methods for market making problems

### Empirical Work
- Analysis of HSBC FX streaming data for client tiering
- Parameter estimation using maximum likelihood
- Clustering techniques for tier identification
