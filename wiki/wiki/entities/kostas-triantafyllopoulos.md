## Biography

Kostas Triantafyllopoulos is a statistician at the University of Sheffield, specializing in Bayesian inference, state-space models, and time series analysis with applications to finance and other domains.

## Research Contributions

### State-Space Models for Finance

1. **Time-Varying Parameter Models**: Developed extensions to standard state-space models that allow parameters to evolve over time, enabling adaptation to changing market conditions

2. **Bayesian Estimation**: Pioneered Bayesian on-line estimation methods that provide uncertainty quantification alongside point estimates

3. **Mean-Reverting Spreads**: Extended Elliott et al. (2005) framework to allow time-varying mean-reversion parameters ([[sources/triantafyllopoulos-2011-mean-reverting-spreads|Triantafyllopoulos & Montana 2011]])

### Methodological Innovations

- Recursive Bayesian updating for real-time applications
- Conditions for mean-reversion in time-varying models
- Convergence properties of on-line estimation algorithms

## Selected Publications

1. Triantafyllopoulos, K., & Montana, G. (2011). Dynamic modeling of mean-reverting spreads for statistical arbitrage. Computational Management Science, 8(1-2), 23-49.

2. Montana, G., Triantafyllopoulos, K., & Tsagaris, T. (2009). Flexible least squares for temporal data mining and statistical arbitrage. Expert Systems with Applications.

## Impact

His work on Bayesian state-space methods has enabled:
- Real-time monitoring of mean-reversion properties
- Stop-loss rules based on parameter uncertainty
- Robust estimation in non-stationary financial markets
