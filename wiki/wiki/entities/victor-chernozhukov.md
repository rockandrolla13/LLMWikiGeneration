# Victor Chernozhukov

**Victor Chernozhukov** is a Professor of Economics at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), widely recognized for his contributions to econometrics, statistics, and machine learning.

## Affiliation

- Massachusetts Institute of Technology
- 50 Memorial Drive, E52-361B, Cambridge, MA 02142, USA
- Email: vchern@mit.edu

## Research Contributions

### Quantile Regression and Distribution Methods
Chernozhukov has made fundamental contributions to quantile regression and distribution regression methods, including:
- Rearrangement methods for monotonizing quantile functions
- Inference methods for quantile regression
- Distribution regression framework

### Conformal Prediction
Co-developed [[concepts/distributional-conformal-prediction|Distributional Conformal Prediction]] (2021) with [[entities/kaspar-wuthrich|Kaspar Wüthrich]] and [[entities/yinchu-zhu|Yinchu Zhu]], which achieves [[concepts/conditional-validity|conditional validity]] by exploiting the [[concepts/probability-integral-transform|probability integral transform]].

### Causal Inference and Machine Learning
Known for the "double/debiased machine learning" framework for causal inference using high-dimensional methods.

## Key Publications

- **Distributional Conformal Prediction** (2021, PNAS) - Conditionally valid prediction intervals via rank permutation
- **Double/Debiased Machine Learning** (2018, Econometrica) - High-dimensional causal inference
- **Inference on Counterfactual Distributions** (2013) - Distribution regression methods

## Impact

Chernozhukov's work bridges theoretical econometrics with practical machine learning applications. His distributional conformal prediction method addresses the fundamental limitation of standard conformal prediction: lack of conditional validity under heteroskedasticity.

## Funding

National Science Foundation support acknowledged in the distributional conformal prediction work.
