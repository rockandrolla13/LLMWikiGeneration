# Kaspar Wüthrich

**Kaspar Wüthrich** is an economist and econometrician at the University of California, San Diego, specializing in econometric methods for prediction and causal inference.

## Affiliation

- Department of Economics, University of California San Diego
- 9500 Gilman Dr., La Jolla, CA 92093, USA
- Email: kwuthrich@ucsd.edu
- Also affiliated with CESifo and the Ifo Institute

## Research Contributions

### Distributional Conformal Prediction
Co-developed [[concepts/distributional-conformal-prediction|Distributional Conformal Prediction]] (2021) with [[entities/victor-chernozhukov|Victor Chernozhukov]] and [[entities/yinchu-zhu|Yinchu Zhu]], a method for constructing conditionally valid prediction intervals using the [[concepts/probability-integral-transform|probability integral transform]].

### Econometric Methods
Research focuses on:
- Prediction intervals for economic forecasting
- Synthetic control methods
- Treatment effect estimation

## Key Publications

- **Distributional Conformal Prediction** (2021, PNAS) - With Chernozhukov and Zhu
