# Anastasios N. Angelopoulos

**Anastasios N. Angelopoulos** is a researcher in [[concepts/conformal-prediction|conformal prediction]] and distribution-free [[concepts/uncertainty-quantification|uncertainty quantification]], known for popularising CP as a practical tool for ML engineers.

## Affiliation

- PhD, EECS, UC Berkeley (advised by Michael I. Jordan and Stephen Bates)
- Currently / recently associated with Berkeley's AI / statistics groups.

## Key Contributions

- Co-author with [[entities/stephen-bates|Stephen Bates]] of "[[sources/angelopoulos-2022-gentle-intro|A Gentle Introduction to Conformal Prediction]]" — the de-facto practitioner reference.
- Co-developer of [[concepts/regularized-adaptive-prediction-sets|RAPS]] (with Bates, Malik, Jordan) for adaptive classification prediction sets at ImageNet scale.
- Co-developer of [[concepts/learn-then-test|Learn Then Test]] for distribution-free control of non-monotone risks.
- Author of widely-used Python libraries for CP.
