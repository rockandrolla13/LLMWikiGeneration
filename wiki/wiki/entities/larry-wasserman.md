# Larry Wasserman

**Larry Wasserman** is University Professor of Statistics and Data Science at Carnegie Mellon University. Foundational contributor to nonparametric and distribution-free inference, including the CMU [[concepts/conformal-prediction|conformal prediction]] school.

## Affiliation

- Department of Statistics & Data Science and Machine Learning Department, Carnegie Mellon University.

## Research Focus

Nonparametric inference, distribution-free statistics, conformal prediction, causal inference, topological data analysis. Author of *All of Statistics* and *All of Nonparametric Statistics* (Springer).

## Publications in This Wiki

- [[sources/lei-2018-distribution-free-regression]] — senior author. The JASA 2018 distribution-free CP-regression paper.

## Notable Theoretical Result

Co-author (with Jing Lei, 2014) of the **distribution-free conditional-coverage impossibility result**: exact conditional coverage cannot be achieved in finite samples without distributional assumptions. The only intervals with this guarantee under no assumptions are vacuous (infinite length almost everywhere). This result is cited by every modern paper on [[concepts/conditional-coverage|conditional coverage]].
