# Sebastian Engelke

**Sebastian Engelke** is a researcher at the Research Institute for Statistics and Information Science, University of Geneva, Switzerland, specializing in [[concepts/extreme-value-theory|extreme value theory]] and its applications to environmental risk assessment.

## Affiliation

- Research Institute for Statistics and Information Science, University of Geneva, Switzerland

## Research Focus

Engelke's research focuses on developing statistical methods for extreme events, including:
- Graphical models for multivariate extremes
- Flood risk assessment and regionalization
- Combining machine learning with extreme value statistics

## Key Contributions

### Extreme Conformal Prediction
Co-developed [[sources/pasche-2025-extreme-conformal|extreme conformal prediction]] with Pasche and Lam, enabling reliable prediction intervals at very high confidence levels for high-impact events.

### Graphical Models for Extremes
Engelke & Hitz (2020) developed graphical models for multivariate extremes, providing sparse structures for modeling dependencies in extreme values.

### Flood Risk Assessment
Contributed to spatial extreme value methods for flood risk:
- Asadi, Davison & Engelke (2015): Extremes on river networks
- Asadi, Engelke & Davison (2018): Optimal regionalization of extreme value distributions
