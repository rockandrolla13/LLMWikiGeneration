# Aymeric Dieuleveut

**Aymeric Dieuleveut** is a researcher specializing in optimization, statistical learning theory, and uncertainty quantification for machine learning.

## Affiliation

- CMAP, CNRS, École Polytechnique
- Institut Polytechnique de Paris, Palaiseau, France

## Research Focus

Dieuleveut's research covers:
- Stochastic optimization algorithms
- Statistical learning theory
- Uncertainty quantification
- Conformal prediction methods

## Key Contributions

### Conformal Prediction with Missing Values

As co-author on [[sources/zaffran-2023-conformal-missing|Zaffran et al. (2023)]], Dieuleveut contributed to developing mask-conditional validity for conformal prediction with incomplete data.

### Adaptive Conformal Inference for Time Series

[[sources/zaffran-2022-aci|Zaffran et al. (2022)]] introduced AgACI (Aggregated Adaptive Conformal Inference), a parameter-free method for time series with distribution drift.

## PhD Supervision

Co-supervisor of [[entities/margaux-zaffran|Margaux Zaffran]]'s PhD thesis on "Post-hoc predictive uncertainty quantification: methods with applications to electricity price forecasting" (École Polytechnique, 2024).
