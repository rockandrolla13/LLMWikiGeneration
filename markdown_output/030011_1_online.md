**Abstract.** The hierarchical credibility model is a model that can accommodate more than one risk factor. This model can better describe the condition of a complex insurance data. Hypothetical mean is used as a quantity to be used for a benchmark of premium calculation. The process of estimating value of hypothetical mean can be done by using the orthogonal projection on one of Hilbert spaces that contains a linear combination of past observations. The estimation of hypothetical mean is a function of structural parameters of the model, which are estimated directly from the data. If the estimation of structural parameters that defines the variance on the upper levels of the hierarchical structure is zero, then that next structural parameters can still be counted with the limit. The methods that can be used to estimate these structural parameters are the Ohlsson method and Bühlmann-Gisler method. In this research, the estimation of hypothetical mean using the hierarchical credibility model and the Bühlmann-Straub model are compared. The result is there is a difference between the usage of both methods when applied on the hierarchical credibility model compared to the Bühlmann-Straub model and the estimation with the hierarchical credibility model defines the situation more realistically. 

**Keywords:** Bühlmann-Straub model, credibility, hierarchical, premium, structural parameter 

## **INTRODUCTION** 

In this life, humans are often faced with uncertainty in various things such as health, personal safety, and other things. This uncertainty makes you feel anxious and wants a protector that protects you from the risks due to this uncertainty. One can do this by joining an insurance program. Insurance is an arrangement between the insurance company and the policyholder [1]. By joining an insurance program, there is a transfer of risk from the policyholder to the insurance company with premium payments in return. Insurance companies need to determine the appropriate amount of premium in order to avoid large losses, one method is the use of credibility theory. 

Credibility theory is a collection of quantitative tools that help insurance companies to adjust the amount of premium in the future based on past observations of each policyholder [2]. By using the credibility theory, the insurance company can make a calculation to determine how credible the past observations of a policyholder are, so that the policyholder will be charged the appropriate premium rate. In general, the credibility theory is divided into the limited fluctuation credibility theory and the greatest accuracy credibility theory, the latter is considered more appropriate to real practice because it is calculated that each policyholder has different risks. 

Two credibility models that are commonly used are the Bühlmann credibility model and the Bühlmann-Straub credibility model. The research that used Bühlmann credibility model can be found in [3], while the research about Bühlmann-Straub credibility model can be found in [4]. In these two models, the risk of each policyholder is different based on risk parameter �� and it is not assumed a prior distribution with specific distributional parameters. The credibility formula under minimum mean square error is obtained in the form of a linear combination of past claims observations. In the Bühlmann credibility model, it is assumed that the mean and variance of claims for each period � for a risk � are the same. The Bühlmann-Straub credibility model is a generalization of the Bühlmann model.

## **CONCLUSION** 

The hierarchical credibility model is general form of Bühlmann-Straub model. The process of estimating credibility premium with hierarchical credibility model using orthogonal projection. The process to find hypothetical mean is top-down procedure, meanwhile the process to find the data compression and credibility factors of each level is bottom-up procedure. The credibility formula is a function of structural parameters. The process to find structural parameters is bottom-up procedure. The problem with zero value estimation of variance structural parameters can be handled with using limit. Two methods to find estimator of structural parameters take effect on hierarchical credibility model, meanwhile on Bühlmann-Straub model may be not.
